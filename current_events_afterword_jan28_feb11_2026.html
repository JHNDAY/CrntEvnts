EPA Endangerment Finding Revocation â–¶
"Largest Deregulatory Action in American History" Dismantles Climate Framework
On February 10, 2026, the White House announced that President Trump and EPA Administrator Lee Zeldin would formalize the rescission of the 2009 endangerment finding on February 12. CNBC reported this represents the Trump administration's biggest attack on climate policy to date.
What is the Endangerment Finding?
  • Origin: 2009 Obama administration determination that greenhouse gases endanger public health and welfare
  • Legal Basis: 2007 Supreme Court ruling in Massachusetts v. EPA that greenhouse gases are air pollutants under Clean Air Act
  • Authority: Provides EPA statutory power to regulate motor vehicle emissions under Section 202(a) of Clean Air Act
  • Impact: Underpins nearly all climate regulations for vehicles, power plants, and other pollution sources
"This will be the largest deregulatory action in American history, and it will save the American people $1.3 trillion in crushing regulations."
â€” White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt, February 10, 2026
