Key Trends and Shifts

Diversification: Trump's coalition became more racially and ethnically diverse, with the share of non-white voters doubling from 11% to 20% since 2016.
Education Gap: Education level remains the strongest predictor of voting behavior, with Trump winning non-college voters by 14 points.
Geographic Realignment: Rural areas became even more Republican, while urban areas remained Democratic. Suburbs became the key battleground.
Generational Shifts: Younger voters showed more variability, with some demographic groups (particularly young Black men and Hispanic men) shifting toward Trump.
Economic Anxiety: Inflation and cost of living concerns drove many voters to Trump, particularly among working-class constituencies across racial lines.
