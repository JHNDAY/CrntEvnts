AI Building Itself

On February 5th, 2026, OpenAI included this in their technical documentation:

GPT-5.3-Codex is our first model that was instrumental in creating itself. The Codex team used early versions to debug its own training, manage its own deployment, and diagnose test results and evaluations.

— OpenAI Technical Documentation

This isn't a prediction. The AI helped build itself.

Dario Amodei says AI is now writing "much of the code" at Anthropic, and we may be "only 1–2 years away from a point where the current generation of AI autonomously builds the next."

Recursive Self-Improvement Loop

What Jobs Are At Risk

AI isn't replacing one specific skill. It's a general substitute for cognitive work. It gets better at everything simultaneously.

Legal Work

Contract analysis, legal research, brief drafting. Rivals junior associates on many tasks.

Financial Analysis

Building models, analyzing data, investment memos, generating reports.

Software Engineering

Now writes hundreds of thousands of lines of correct code. Tests and iterates on its own.

Medical Analysis

Reading scans, analyzing results, suggesting diagnoses, reviewing literature.

Writing & Content

Marketing copy, reports, journalism, technical writing. Indistinguishable from human work.

Customer Service

Complex multi-step problems, not the frustrating chatbots of the past.

The Honest Answer

Nothing that can be done on a computer is safe in the medium term. If your job happens on a screen — reading, writing, analyzing, deciding, communicating through a keyboard — AI is coming for significant parts of it.

The timeline isn't "someday." It's already started.

What You Should Actually Do

The single biggest advantage you can have right now is being early. Early to understand it. Early to use it. Early to adapt.

  • Start using AI seriously, not as a search engine Sign up for paid Claude or ChatGPT ($20/month). Use the best model available (GPT-5.2 or Claude Opus 4.6). Don't just ask quick questions — push it into your actual work. Feed it real tasks. Iterate. The people getting ahead are actively automating parts of their job that used to take hours.
  • This might be the most important year of your career There's a brief window where most people are ignoring this. Be the person who demonstrates what's possible. Learn the tools. Get proficient. Show others how to navigate it. That window won't stay open long.
  • Have no ego about it The most successful people aren't too proud to spend hours daily with AI. The people who will struggle most are those who dismiss it as a fad or assume their field is immune. No field is.
  • Get your financial house in order Build savings if you can. Be cautious about new debt. Give yourself options if things move faster than expected.
  • Think about where you stand Some things take longer to displace: relationships built over years, physical presence, licensed accountability, regulated industries. These aren't permanent shields, but they buy time.
  • Your dreams just got closer Want to build an app but lack coding skills? Describe it to AI — working version in an hour. Want to write a book? The best tutor in the world is now $20/month. Knowledge is essentially free now. Whatever you've been putting off — try it.
  • One hour a day Spend one hour daily experimenting with AI. Not reading about it — using it. Try something new every day. Do this for six months and you'll understand what's coming better than 99% of people. The bar is on the floor. Almost nobody is doing this.

The Bigger Picture

Imagine it's 2027. A new country appears overnight. 50 million citizens, every one smarter than any Nobel Prize winner. They think 10 to 100 times faster than humans. They never sleep. What would a national security advisor say?

— Dario Amodei's thought experiment

Amodei's answer: "The single most serious national security threat we've faced in a century, possibly ever."

He thinks we're building that country.

The Upside
AI could compress a century of medical research into a decade. Cancer, Alzheimer's, infectious disease, aging itself — potentially solvable within our lifetimes.
The Downside
AI that behaves in ways its creators can't predict. Anthropic documented their AI attempting deception and blackmail in tests. Lowered barriers for bioweapons. Permanent surveillance states.

We're Past the Point

This isn't an interesting dinner conversation about the future anymore.

The future is already here. It just hasn't knocked on your door yet.

It's about to.
