03 Supreme Court & Other Legal Issues 43 mentions ▼
Learning Resources v. Trump — IEEPA Tariffs Unconstitutional
The Supreme Court's 6-3 ruling (Feb. 20) held that IEEPA does not authorize the president to impose tariffs. Chief Justice Roberts invoked the "major questions" doctrine, finding Congress had not clearly delegated this "core power of the purse." Justices Gorsuch and Barrett questioned the non-delegation implications; Alito and Thomas dissented on separation of powers grounds. The ruling voided $166B in collected tariffs.
NYT Politics Politico NPR Politics CNN
CIT Blocks Section 122 Tariffs — May 7 Ruling
The Court of International Trade dealt a second blow to the White House on May 7, ruling 2-1 that the alternative 10% global tariffs imposed under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 were unjustified — the statute requires a "large and serious balance-of-payments deficit," which the court found the administration had not adequately demonstrated. This sets up another Supreme Court confrontation as Section 301 investigations proceed.
NYT Economy CNN Business Politico BBC Business
Alien Enemies Act — Courts Block Peacetime Deportations
Federal courts rejected the administration's use of the 1798 Alien Enemies Act to deport Venezuelan nationals during peacetime, finding no armed invasion or predatory incursion. A split three-judge panel upheld the lower court injunction. The administration is appealing, but the decisions have significantly constrained the deportation pipeline. NPR and the NYT covered the constitutional and historical dimensions of invoking a wartime statute for civil immigration enforcement.
NPR Immigration NYT US Guardian Immigration CNN
Courts Freeze HHS Restructuring
