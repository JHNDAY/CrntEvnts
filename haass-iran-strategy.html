The Trump administration has pursued three military and diplomatic strategies against Iran—all unsuccessful. Iran has demonstrated resilience, strategic sophistication, and the ability to inflict meaningful costs on the U.S. and its regional partners. The core question: what comes next?

Root Causes of Failure

Economic Miscalculation

The administration overestimated the persuasive power of economic incentives and underestimated nationalism, pride, and anger in Iranian decision-making.

Institutional Strength

Iran's regime, established in 1979, proved far more institutionalized and resilient than anticipated. Public uprisings were ruthlessly repressed.

Military Endurance

Iran demonstrated sustained ability to absorb punishment and inflict costs through regional attacks and Strait closure—one-fifth of global energy flows.

Iran's Three Principal Sources of Leverage must be systematically reduced through a long-term, comprehensive strategy that addresses energy independence, regional defense, nuclear development, economic pressure, and the inadequate current Memorandum of Understanding.

The Core Leverage Points

What Iran Controls

The Strait of Hormuz — One-fifth of the world's oil and gas transits here. Iran can close it or charge tolls, creating cascading global economic consequences.
Regional Aggression Capability — Iran can attack Arab neighbors' energy and water infrastructure via drones, missiles, cyber, and armed proxies, with potential to transform a limited crisis into global economic calamity.
Nuclear Weapons Pathway — If developed, nuclear weapons grant Iran regional impunity, ability to empower proxies against Israel and others, and could trigger a regional arms race.
The Five-Point Comprehensive Strategy

1 Reduce Strait Dependence

Accelerate U.S. and global energy production, reduce demand, expand alternatives, build pipelines bypassing the Strait, and increase strategic reserves. Undermines Iran's leverage and reduces impact of closures.

2 Enhance Regional Defenses

Work with Ukraine, Israel, and partners to defend energy and water infrastructure against drones, missiles, cyber, and terrorism. Effective defense is essential for credible deterrence.

3 Prevent Nuclear Development

Pursue diplomacy with international inspections to verify compliance. If dangerous activity detected, be prepared to use military force against suspected sites as necessary.

4 Reimpose Economic Sanctions

Restrict oil sales on open markets. Link any sanctions relief to progress in nuclear negotiations. Target regime revenue streams while preserving negotiation incentives.

5 Scrap the MOU

Current accord gives Iran formal cover for Strait control, provides resources without limiting nuclear/conventional arms/proxies. Only renegotiate when Iran's leverage is substantially diminished.

✓ Economic Pressure Principle

Iran's economic weakness remains primary vulnerability. Economic relief must be heavily and specifically conditioned. Never abandon possibility that pressure could lead to policy moderation.

"The operative adjectives of the strategy outlined here are long-term and comprehensive. The only safe and effective way to pressure Iran is by steadily reducing the importance of the Strait and Iran's ability to damage its neighbors."

The Strategic Dilemma

Military Options Are Severely Constrained

U.S. military action remains inherently limited because Iran can escalate beyond sustainable costs—threatening global oil supplies or attacking regional infrastructure—potentially triggering worldwide economic calamity. This means military action will remain performative rather than decisive unless coupled with structural strategies that reduce Iran's leverage over time.

Convoying Ships

Costly, dangerous, and inefficient. Does not guarantee the Strait remains open to shipping.

Seizing Kharg Island

Difficult, incurs casualties, and still fails to guarantee open Strait access. Not a sustainable solution.

Attacking Military Targets

Has been shown ineffective; more of the same will not change the fundamental equation. Iran absorbs punishment and escalates regionally.
