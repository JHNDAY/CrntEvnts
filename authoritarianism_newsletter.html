THE AUTHORITARIAN WATCH
A Newsletter on Rising Authoritarianism in America
July 1-25, 2025 Issue - Summary Points
INSTITUTIONAL BREAKDOWN
Supreme Court enables executive overreach: July 14 ruling allows Trump to gut Department of Education despite congressional opposition, marking major expansion of presidential power
Political scientists warn of authoritarianism: Over 500 scholars surveyed believe U.S. is moving swiftly toward competitive authoritarianism; Harvard's Steven Levitsky states "we are no longer living in a liberal democracy"
Trust in institutions cratering: Washington Post analysis shows Trump leveraging institutional distrust to position himself as sole authority
BUDGET OPACITY & CONGRESSIONAL BYPASS
Russell Vought refuses budget transparency: OMB Director indicates "we won't release a budget this year," threatens illegal "impoundment" of funds despite 1974 law prohibiting practice
Republicans voting for unpopular bills: Nearly 1,000-page reconciliation bill rammed through without debate; Speaker Johnson's gavel presentation to Trump symbolized congressional capitulation
Senate Republicans lack information: Appropriations Chair Susan Collins admits "lacking the level of detail needed to make right decisions"
FEDERAL RESERVE UNDER ASSAULT
Trump threatens to fire Powell: July 15 meeting with GOP lawmakers where Trump gestured at claimed "termination letter" for Fed Chair
Coordinated pressure campaign: White House advisers launching multi-pronged attacks; Russell Vought accusing Powell of lying to Congress about headquarters renovation
Powell refuses to resign: Fed Chair tells associates "no chance" of stepping down, argues resignation would undermine Fed independence
Market warnings ignored: Deutsche Bank estimates 3-4% dollar drop within 24 hours if Powell fired; financial markets briefly tumbled on firing rumors
WEAPONIZING GOVERNMENT AGENCIES
ICE escalates violent raids: California cannabis farm raids using less-lethal ammunition and tear gas; 200 arrested including disabled U.S. veteran George Retes, whose family cannot locate him
FEMA deliberately sabotaged: Kristi Noem terminated call center contracts during Texas floods, causing response rate to plummet from 99.7% to 15.9% within two days
National Guard deployed against states: Trump called up military forces in Los Angeles against California governor's wishes, testing federal-state power dynamics
ACADEMIC & MEDIA INTIMIDATION
Universities targeted for funding cuts: Billions withheld from Harvard, Princeton, Columbia; scholars fear government retribution for research grants
Media capitulation accelerates: ABC and CBS agree to $15M and $16M settlements to Trump lawsuits; Washington Post and LA Times refused Harris endorsements
Academic self-censorship: NPR interviewee requested anonymity after initial quote, fearing administration retaliation
EXPERT WARNINGS
Concentration camps as slave labor sites: Historian Timothy Snyder warns Trump's detention facilities could become sites for forced labor under "owner responsibility" model
Immigration enforcement as authoritarian pathway: Legal experts note Trump using ICE raids to test boundaries of power consolidation and political enforcement
Historical precedents activated: Heather Cox Richardson draws parallels to authoritarian consolidation patterns in other countries
