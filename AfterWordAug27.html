Historical Perspective: Women's Suffrage and Political Evolution ▶
105th Anniversary of 19th Amendment Prompts Historical Reflection
Smithsonian Magazine published comprehensive analysis of the 19th Amendment's legacy, placing American women's suffrage in global historical context.
The analysis noted that while the United States granted women voting rights in 1920, some countries had already extended suffrage in the 1890s, and many Black women and women of color remained disenfranchised until the 1965 Voting Rights Act.
Global Women's Suffrage Timeline:
  • 1890s: First countries grant women voting rights
  • 1918: Britain extends limited women's suffrage
  • 1920: U.S. ratifies 19th Amendment
  • 1965: Voting Rights Act ensures access for all American women
  • Historical impact: 26 million women gained voting rights in 1920
"The right of citizens of the United States to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any state on account of sex."
— 19th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution
The Atlantic explored how women's political organizing during the suffrage era offers lessons for contemporary coalition building and grassroots activism.
Sources: Smithsonian Magazine, The Atlantic
