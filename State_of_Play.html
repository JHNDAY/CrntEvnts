📋 Status of Major Policy Initiatives
Where each flagship agenda item stands as of April 12, 2026
Fragile
Iran War (Operation Epic Fury) → Two-Week Ceasefire
2,000+ strikes in 38 days; ceasefire brokered by Pakistan; Strait of Hormuz still not fully open; Iran's nuclear stockpile unresolved.
▼

The 38-day air campaign concluded with a ceasefire brokered by Pakistan's PM Sharif — reached with 88 minutes to spare before Trump's self-imposed deadline. Both sides immediately claimed victory. Iran said it accepted on its own terms; the White House said Iran "begged" for peace.

Iran kept firing until the last day and the Strait of Hormuz remains "effectively closed" — Iran is imposing per-ship tolls. By April 9, only three ships had transited since the ceasefire.

Iran's post-war demands — lifting all sanctions, U.S. military withdrawal, reparations, and the right to enrich uranium — are described as far beyond what Washington can accept. VP Vance is leading talks in Islamabad with Witkoff and Kushner.

Iran's nuclear stockpile — sufficient for a weapon — was not resolved. Trump said it would be "perfectly taken care of." A senior Israeli official told NYT the U.S. assured Israel it would insist on uranium removal.

Supreme Leader Khamenei was killed; the assassination of a foreign head of state without a declaration of war or imminent-threat finding has no modern legal precedent.

Sources: NPR CNN NPR (ceasefire) Atlantic Haaretz
Struck Down
Tariff Regime → IEEPA Ruled Unconstitutional; Replacement Now Challenged
SCOTUS voided IEEPA tariffs (Feb. 20); $166B refund owed. New Section 122 tariffs face fresh legal challenge filed Apr. 10.
▼

In a landmark 6-3 ruling (Feb. 20), Chief Justice Roberts — joined by Gorsuch, Barrett, and the three liberal justices — held that IEEPA does not grant the president tariff authority. NYT called it "the most significant restraint on presidential trade power in a generation."

$166 billion in refunds are owed to 330,000+ businesses. Interest accrues at $650M per month. Over 2,000 lawsuits from FedEx, Costco, Nissan, L'Oreal, and others now clog the Court of International Trade.

Trump's replacement regime — a 10–15% global surcharge under Section 122 of the 1974 Trade Act — faced a new legal challenge on April 10, filed by 24 states and small businesses. Plaintiffs argue the law's "balance-of-payments" requirement is not met in a floating-currency economy.

Sources: NYT Politico CNN Guardian
Under Strain
Mass Deportation Campaign → Continues, But DHS Funding Has Lapsed
68,000 in detention (record); DHS unfunded since Feb. 14; ICE relies on Medicaid data; CECOT deportations ongoing.
▼

As of April 8, 68,000 people are in immigration detention — a record — as the DHS funding lapse prevents full case processing. 14 deaths in detention facilities in Q1 2026 have been documented.

ICE has direct access to Medicaid home address data for 28 states. A third of adult immigrants had already skipped or postponed healthcare in 2025 (KFF/NYT poll).

Deportation flights to El Salvador's CECOT mega-prison continue. A critical DNA database used to identify deceased migrants has disappeared. Courts have temporarily blocked TPS revocation for 350,000 Haitians and 6,000 Syrians.

DHS has been unfunded since Feb. 14. TSA agents have missed paychecks; 300+ officers have resigned. The Senate's partial DHS bill left ICE unfunded — creating a political standoff within Trump's own majority.

Sources: CNN (Medicaid/ICE) NPR Guardian Politico
In Disarray
HHS / RFK Jr. Agenda → Multiple Court Blocks; CDC Leadership Ousted
CDC director fired; ACIP restructuring reversed by courts; ACA premium spikes; measles resurgence; rural Medicaid cuts biting.
▼

RFK forced CDC Director Monarez out; the White House fired her when she refused to leave. Three senior CDC officials resigned. RFK's replacement ACIP (vaccine advisory panel) was staffed with anti-vaccine activists — then reversed by a federal court ruling RFK exceeded his authority without proper administrative procedure.

An Oregon federal judge blocked RFK's declaration that gender-affirming treatments are "unsafe and ineffective" for bypassing required rulemaking procedures.

ACA enhanced subsidies expired at end of 2025, causing dramatic premium spikes — disproportionately in Republican-dominated states. KFF polling: healthcare costs are the top economic concern for 44% of 2026 voters. A measles resurgence is underway.

$137B in rural Medicaid cuts have begun to reach communities. 1.4 million lawfully present immigrants projected to lose health coverage under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (effective 2027).

Sources: NYT NPR (Medicare/TPS) CNN Guardian
Stalled
Ukraine Peace Push → Geneva Talks Collapsed; Russia Still Advancing
Third round of U.S.-Ukraine-Russia talks collapsed in two hours; Zelensky calls U.S. framework "not fair"; Iran war absorbed all diplomatic bandwidth.
▼

The third round of trilateral talks in Geneva collapsed after just two hours on day two. Russia declared a 32-hour Easter ceasefire then immediately violated it with drone strikes. Zelensky: the U.S. "demands more from Ukraine than from Russia."

The U.S. peace framework — requiring Ukraine to cede territories Russia has not yet captured — is seen by Kyiv as capitulation. Russia simultaneously violated its own Easter ceasefire, killing civilians in Odesa and Kherson.

The Iran war has effectively sidelined Ukraine: Witkoff and Kushner are now in Pakistan. The Atlantic argues this gives Russia a strategic windfall — time to grind in Donbas while U.S. attention and leverage are consumed elsewhere. Harvard's Graham Allison has publicly warned Russia will likely seize all Donbas within a year at the current pace.

Sources: NYT Politico BBC Atlantic Guardian
