Trump's 7 Most Authoritarian Moves

Analysis of Power Consolidation in the Second Term

Based on CNN Politics Analysis - August 13, 2025

1

Putting Military on US Soil

Unprecedented deployment of National Guard in Washington DC and Marines in Los Angeles. Trump is increasingly talking about deploying active-duty military domestically and expanding to other cities, militarizing the homeland like never before.

Impact: Breaks longstanding norms about military deployment on US soil, raising concerns about politicization of military and targeting of American citizens.
Critical Threat
2

Emergency Tariff Powers

Claiming emergency powers over trade - a constitutional prerogative of Congress. Constantly shifting tariff terms to bargain with other countries, effectively wielding ever-changing taxation on American businesses and consumers.

Impact: Neutered Congress and runs the country like an all-powerful executive on trade policy, with courts still determining if he's exceeded authority.
High Threat
3

Investigations of Opponents

Launched investigations against key figures from Russia investigation, first impeachment, January 6 investigations, and personal criminal cases. Trump personally pushes for many of these probes, breaking with longstanding practice.

Impact: Sends message to potential whistleblowers and investigators: "If you scrutinize him, we'll go after you" - a recipe for people keeping quiet.
High Threat
4

MAGA-fying Independent Functions

Fired Bureau of Labor Statistics head over disliked jobs report, then nominated MAGA loyalist. Commerce Secretary called independence of federal statistics "nonsense." Similar pressure on Federal Reserve and other nonpartisan officials.

Impact: Erodes expectation of independence from officials who deliver data, adding political pressure to apolitical government functions.
Medium Threat
5

Coercion of Institutions

Used bully pulpit and executive powers to demonstrate dominance over media, universities, and law firms. These institutions have made remarkable concessions, calculating it's best not to be on Trump's bad side.

Impact: Sets new precedents that impact other institutions and embolden Trump. He openly gloats about institutions "bending" and saying "Where do I sign?"
Medium Threat
6

Ignoring Congress/TikTok

Despite Congress passing bipartisan law requiring TikTok divestment and Supreme Court upholding it, Trump keeps giving TikTok extensions without clear authority. Also disregards congressionally appropriated spending.

Impact: President effectively choosing to disregard the law because he can, despite supposed urgent national security concerns about Chinese data mining.
High Threat
7

Skirting Due Process on Deportations

Invoked wartime Alien Enemies Act to quickly deport undocumented immigrants. Led to wrongful deportations and attempted deportations blocked by courts. Administration ignored court orders to turn around airplanes.

Impact: Calls into question authority of rule of law and courts, with Trump poking and prodding to force constitutional crisis.
Critical Threat

The Consolidation of Power

These seven moves represent a systematic effort to consolidate executive power and test democratic boundaries. From militarizing domestic law enforcement to ignoring congressional authority, the pattern shows Trump "bulldozed the obstacles that often stood in his way in his first term" and is governing with "almost single-minded pursuit of more authority." Whether viewed as necessary leadership or authoritarian overreach, these actions are "recasting the balance of power in American government and pushing us in a more authoritarian direction."
