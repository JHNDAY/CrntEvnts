Topic I · Major Discussion
Iran: A War of Choice, a Depleted Arsenal & a Superior Opponent
GeorgeOpening Frame
The planning for Iran resembles a golf coach's advice: worry only about the current swing, see where the ball lands, then worry about the next shot. Fine for golf. Catastrophically inadequate for foreign policy. We bombed the hell out of Iran and then saw what happened. I think we're paying the price. It's hard not to sympathize with Iran — their strategy and expertise at the negotiating table are far superior to ours. We're trying to reclaim what we already had in the JCPOA, which Trump threw out in 2018.
Michael BergerIran's Audacity
Iran's response to Trump's naval blockade during the ceasefire: they'll let ships through — but only through Iranian waters, at a toll of $2 million per vessel, with the rest of the strait reportedly mined. "We can't dismiss the ingenuity and audacity of the Iranians." Meanwhile, our negotiating team is Kushner, Witkoff, and Vance. Iran brought 70 scientists and diplomats.
Bob FishmanWalk-Away Advantage
In any negotiation — real estate or foreign affairs — the critical variable is walk-away advantage: the party who can leave without a deal holds the leverage. Iran is hanging in there. They know the midterms are coming. They read Trump's poll numbers. And Trump's pattern is clear: he'll declare victory regardless of the outcome, put lipstick on the pig, and call it a win. He's trying to find any exit now.
Hal ShearWho's Actually Running Iran
Khamenei's son was badly wounded in the bombing — lost a leg, severely burned, can barely speak. But he fought as a young man in the Iran-Iraq war, and his wartime comrades are now the surviving generals. They consult with him via text, but they are effectively running Iran's strategy collectively. "We've always assumed there's a dictator here — but that's not the case."
Group Consensus: Iran Has the Upper Hand
Ralph Loomis: This is a war of choice — Trump has never provided a coherent rationale. The bombing has pushed Iranian leadership toward radicalization, not regime change. U.S. munitions are depleting rapidly. Iran's social media, amplified globally, is shaping world opinion against America.

Sally Fleschner: Iran doesn't even need to develop nuclear weapons — they can acquire access through Russia, North Korea, Pakistan, or India. Trump's framework simply doesn't account for this.

Francesca Nannetti: Iran negotiated the JCPOA over 20 months with Kerry, Sherman, and seasoned professionals. Kushner and Witkoff are "flying by the seat of their pants."
"When I see our negotiating team is Vance, Kushner, and Witkoff — and they bring 70 scientists and diplomats — it's almost hard not to root for them." — George, co-moderator
Topic II
The New Business Model: Corruption, Roy Cohn & the Big Lie
John DayThe Carousel of Life
Trump reached out and grabbed the gold ring. Became president. And his new business model is corruption — pursued on a scale that, as he would say, has never been seen in the world. The people around him understand that his decisions can make them money. Their business plan: do whatever he says. The UAE deals represent perhaps the most egregious case — personal gain, then advocacy for taxpayer money flowing to that same partner.
Ralph LoomisOn Maggie Haberman & Roy Cohn
Haberman's book documents Trump's actual business record: buildings that didn't make it, a preferred tactic of simply not paying contractors — plumbers, electricians, carpenters — with long lists of suits to prove it. His biggest advisor, Roy Cohn, gave him the strategy that became foundational: "When you go into the public forum, lie. They won't know it's a lie. Keep lying the same way — eventually, they'll believe you."
John DayRoy Cohn's Other Mentee
Let's not forget that another distinguished mentee of Roy Cohn was Joe McCarthy — whose downfall was finally prompted by Joseph Welch of Hale and Dorr: "Have you no sense of decency, sir?"
Recommended Reading
Francesca Nannetti recommended Lucky Loser (two NY Times reporters, ~2 years ago): Trump's pre-political history in New York, detailed failure in real estate, and his father's history. Key point: The Apprentice created the myth of competence. The Mussolini principle predates Cohn: the bigger the lie, the more it's repeated, the more people believe it.
Topic III · High-Energy Discussion
The Hilton Ballroom: Assassination Attempt or Something Else?
Sally FleschnerRaising the Question
Sally opened with a confession: she's been "completely glued" to this story — and something about it doesn't ring true. Trump's reaction was strangely blasé. The attacker never actually entered the ballroom. She noted the contrast: RFK Jr. being rushed out while his wife scrambled to keep up in a strapless dress, vs. Stephen Miller carefully protecting his pregnant wife. Jimmy Kimmel's joke three days earlier — that Melania had "an expectant widow's glow" — was now being used to press ABC/Disney through FCC review of broadcast licenses.
Ralph LoomisThe Anatomy of a Conspiracy Theory
Within 2 minutes of the incident, social media was flooded — roughly 80% claiming it was staged, primarily from white women commenters. Two hours later, one commentator had 800,000 followers reading along. A network reached Nick Fuentes: "I threw that out there — I get a lot of comments. Did this happen? I don't think so. But it's a good way to stir people up." No one had any actual information. The claim spread on its own momentum.
Michael BergerThe Real Issue
This is a genuine security problem — not a conspiracy. There have been 40–50 school and college shootings this year already. The Second Amendment allowed a man with no hunting background to acquire a sophisticated weapon. Instead of building a $200M ballroom, we should spend that money on mental health and gun control. That's the argument no Democrat in Congress has adequately made.
Francesca NannettiThe Larger Security Failure
What truly struck her: the president, vice president, Speaker of the House — the entire line of succession — were all present in the same room. "If somebody really had wanted to do something in a very professional way… the possibility of eliminating so many people is astounding." One designated survivor and the entire government is exposed.
Topic IV · Mark Seliber
A Tale of Two Drafts: Medicine vs. Football
March 20 vs. April 23–25
Medical Match Day · March 20
41,482 medical school graduates opened envelopes to learn their hospital residency placements. No cameras. No crowds.
NFL Draft · April 23–25 · Pittsburgh
257 college students learned on national TV which team they'll play for. 13.2 million TV viewers. ~300,000 in-person attendees daily. Pittsburgh public schools closed Thursday and Friday.
Mark SeliberThe Verdict
"Somehow, I think the 41,482 residents will collectively have a more positive future impact on the world than the 257 football players." A championship team can unite a city across polarization — but the NFL's hold on American attention has become disproportionate in a way that reveals where our priorities actually lie.

Topic V
Kevin Warsh & the Future of the Fed
Who Is Kevin Warsh?
  • Former Fed governor — genuine financial markets expertise
  • Not an economist; a financially sophisticated political operator
  • Recent positioning has signaled alignment with Trump administration
  • Thom Tillis (NC) removed his hold after DOJ dropped Powell prosecution
  • Distinguished from Hegseth, Patel, Bondi: he actually has qualifications
Bob FishmanThe Wink
It beggars the imagination that Trump would nominate him without some understanding — even just a wink — about what Warsh would do on interest rates. Trump has been burned too many times. He has assurances, even if nothing is in writing.
Eric PackerPolitically Savvy Lapdog
Warsh is very savvy — he knows how to play the political game. But he is not an economist. Powell was essentially inoculated against political pressure. Warsh will be far more susceptible to arguments from Trump and conservative economists. Qualified on paper, yes. But this is a political hire — and you'll see him respond accordingly.
Michael BergerPowell's Record
In April, Powell received the Kennedy Library's Profiles in Courage award — and Michael voted for him for it. He holds his Fed governor seat until 2028, and Trump has been pressing him to resign. The Federal Reserve is supposed to be independent. "In many things — even the Constitution — Trump doesn't feel he should worry about things that aren't truly legal."
Linda FritzThe Question
Will Powell resign from the Fed board even after his chairmanship ends? Tradition suggests Fed chairs don't remain as governors. But Linda hopes he stays — and the prosecution, though paused, hasn't been formally cleared. They can refile.

The Thread That Ran Through Everything
From Iran to the Federal Reserve to the ballroom to the soap opera: the group returned again and again to a single organizing question — what happens when the institutions designed to constrain executive power encounter a president who regards those constraints as optional? The JCPOA was abandoned. Powell was prosecuted. The FCC moved on Disney. The Fed's independence is being tested. The designated-survivor protocol exists precisely for scenarios like the Hilton ballroom. None of these safeguards were designed for an adversary who treats them as suggestions.

Bob Fishman's negotiation framework applies beyond Iran: who has the walk-away advantage? In most of these contests, the answer at the moment is Trump — because the countervailing institutions have not yet found their Joseph Welch.
