03 — Oil as Weapon & the Potential Acceleration Toward Non-Carbon Energy
Iran's Leverage
Oil as a Weapon of Attrition
  • Iran, not the US, likely decides when this war ends — they control supply disruption duration
  • Strait of Hormuz closure: the "thought experiment" academics said could never happen — has happened
  • Oman (previous Iran–West broker) struck; regional off-ramps eliminated
  • Tankers are "sitting ducks" — infrastructure takes years to rebuild, Hormuz could be mined
  • Iran has decades of stockpiles, dispersed production — bombing does not destroy their capacity
  • Deals to let China through the Strait quietly? Iran's geopolitical leverage is maximum now
  • Continued high oil prices sustain Putin's Russia, keeping Ukraine quagmire going (Dave Chosiad)
Fortress America Temptation
The Self-Sufficiency Gambit
  • US is world's largest oil producer; with Canada + Venezuela = hemispheric sufficiency
  • At $100+/barrel, Texas and Alberta wells that were uneconomic become viable
  • Administration could cap domestic pump price politically to avoid $5/gallon pain
  • But: domestic oil at $120/bbl is inherently inflationary — raises price of everything (Bob Halpin)
  • Breaks below $100/bbl → domestic wells go offline again; no stability
  • "Europe, that's your problem" (Shapiro): isolationism + one major war = schizophrenic foreign policy
  • Trump's "Fortress America" instinct clashes with the Feb 28 decision to enter the war
The Energy Inflection Point
Oil Crisis as Green Energy Accelerant
  • $4–$5/gallon gas is a powerful economic signal toward EVs, hybrids, solar (Shapiro, Halpin)
  • Electricity: only 4% traded across borders — inherently local & sovereign vs. oil's global chains
  • Greening the grid = strategic flexibility; renewables can come online faster than oil pipelines
  • "Price goes up like a rocket and drops like a feather" — if sustained, changes consumer behavior durably
  • Decarbonization also defunds authoritarian petrostates: Russia, Iran, Gulf monarchies (McKnight)
  • Solar on rooftops becomes economically attractive at sustained $120/bbl
  • Key question for the course: demand destruction vs. switching to alternatives vs. new fossil sources
The Political Counter-Force
Trump Admin's Fossil Fuel Lock-In
  • Energy Secretary Chris Wright: "reliability" framing — dismisses intermittency of solar/wind
  • Chris Wright lied about Hormuz reopening → caused $billions in market manipulation
  • Admin dismantling green energy programs, opposing grid modernization
  • Political capture by utilities: "I sell electricity to you — I don't want your rooftop solar"
  • Battery-making, solar PV, EVs — industries born after globalization, never built domestically
  • Sulfur from disrupted refineries also disrupts EV battery supply chain (cobalt, nickel, copper)
  • DOGE cuts $200M film budgets but week-1 war cost = $11.3B — fiscal contradiction (McKnight)

The session surfaced a profound irony: the very war launched partly to neutralize Iran's nuclear threat may have made Iran's nuclear calculations more aggressive, unified its population, depleted Western missile stocks, exposed Gulf Monarchy fragility, and created a window for China over Taiwan — while simultaneously generating the highest oil prices in years. The question the group posed for coming weeks: does this crisis destroy demand for fossil fuels (pushing toward EVs and renewables), or does it lock in new fossil supply from the Western hemisphere? The answer likely depends on how long the disruption lasts — and whether political will can overcome the fossil fuel industry's grip on policy. As McKnight put it: "It's going to be the Iranians who decide when this is finished."
