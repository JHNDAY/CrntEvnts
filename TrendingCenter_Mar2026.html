01 Middle East — US-Israel War on Iran 62 mentions ▼
War Begins — Khamenei Killed in Surprise Strike
On February 28, the US and Israel launched coordinated strikes on Iran — killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei — with no congressional authorization and just as nuclear negotiations were reportedly nearing a deal. The New York Times and CNN both noted that Pentagon briefers acknowledged to congressional staff that Iran had not been planning to strike US forces first, undercutting the administration's "imminent threat" justification.
New York Times CNN Al Jazeera BBC
Elementary School Strike — US Accountability Crisis
The New York Times reported on a preliminary US military investigation finding that a Tomahawk missile struck an Iranian elementary school on the first day of the war, killing at least 175 people according to Iranian state media. Outdated targeting information was blamed. NPR noted that the Trump administration had slashed 90% of Pentagon teams responsible for reducing civilian casualties before the war began.
New York Times NPR CNN
Strait of Hormuz — Global Energy Crisis
Near-total halt of Strait of Hormuz traffic — through which 20% of world oil passes — sent Brent crude past $115/barrel. The International Energy Agency called it "the largest supply disruption in global oil market history" and urged governments to implement emergency demand-reduction measures including remote work and reduced highway speeds. Goldman Sachs warned high prices could persist through 2027.
New York Times CNN NPR BBC
Casualties Mount — Iran, Lebanon, Iraq
By mid-March, Iran's UN ambassador reported over 1,300 civilians killed in Iran including at least 206 children, alongside 1,122 military personnel. Lebanon's health ministry confirmed at least 886 killed since Israel began striking on March 2; 47 killed in Iraq including a French soldier. Thirteen US service members died — seven from enemy fire — plus a mid-March plane crash carrying six Air National Guard members.
CNN NPR Al Jazeera The Guardian
Lebanon Ground War Opens — Hezbollah Front
Israel launched ground operations in southern Lebanon beginning March 2, as Hezbollah attack waves intensified. CNN obtained the first live American television report from Tehran on March 6. Israel struck the Lebanese capital Beirut and ordered evacuations across 52 settlements in southern Lebanon, opening a second major front to the conflict that Haaretz warned could destabilize any future peace framework.
CNN Haaretz BBC Al Jazeera
Trump Claims Iran Sought Ceasefire — Iran Denies
Trump told NBC News on March 14 that Iran was "ready to make a deal" but terms weren't good enough. The next day, Iran's Foreign Minister Araghchi publicly denied that Iran had requested a ceasefire. The Atlantic reported Trump floated an off-ramp — expressing willingness to speak with Iran's new leadership — but the administration sent contradictory signals within the same news cycle.
The Atlantic NPR New York Times
FCC Threatens Broadcaster Licenses over War Coverage
FCC Chair Brendan Carr warned on March 14 that broadcasters covering the Iran war in ways the administration deemed "fake news" risked losing their licenses. CNN and NPR were specifically named by Trump. The BBC and Guardian covered Democratic lawmakers condemning Carr's threats as unconstitutional censorship and a direct attack on press freedom.
NPR CNN The Guardian BBC

Ford's deployment — now in its 10th month — could become the longest US carrier deployment since Vietnam. 05 ICE Operations — Enforcement, Surveillance & Courts 28 mentions ▼
DHS Mass Surveillance of Activists Documented
An NPR investigation published March 4, drawing on dozens of interviews and court documents, detailed how DHS uses facial recognition, license plate readers, and the AI-powered "ELITE" app (made by Palantir) to track and intimidate both immigrants and US citizen activists. One ICE agent testified under oath that the app shows likely home addresses of deportation targets "similar to Google Maps," triggering legal challenges in multiple circuits.
NPR The Guardian CNN
Medicaid Data Weaponized for Deportation — 22-State Lawsuit
Following a December court ruling, Medicaid enrollment data may now be shared with ICE without restrictions in 28 states. NPR reported families — including parents of children with serious disabilities — were dropping health coverage to avoid deportation risk. Twenty-two states filed suit to block the data-sharing arrangement. The Guardian called it "an unprecedented merger of the healthcare and immigration enforcement systems."
NPR The Guardian Politico
Operation Metro Surge — Judge Orders Counsel Rights Restored
A Trump-appointed federal judge ruled that ICE's Minneapolis "Metro Surge" operations had "all but extinguished" detainees' constitutional right to counsel — by flying people to Texas within hours and blocking attorney access. The judge ordered ICE to immediately restore legal access for detainees. NPR and the New York Times covered Kristi Noem's subsequent Senate testimony about the operation and her later removal as DHS Secretary.
New York Times NPR Politico
ICE Detention Expansion — 73,000 Held, Record Deaths
A February report by the American Immigration Council documented ICE detention growing to 73,000 people — a 75% increase in one year. Internal ICE memos revealed plans for $38.3 billion in expansion to 92,600 beds including eight mega-centers, to be funded through the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act." 2025 was the deadliest year for ICE detention on record; the Guardian and NPR reported 2026 is trending worse.
The Guardian NPR New York Times
Palestinian Activist Released — Tribal Members Detained
An immigration judge ordered the release of a Palestinian activist whose detention had drawn wide condemnation. 07 Supreme Court & Other Legal Issues 22 mentions ▼
Birthright Citizenship — Trump v. Barbara Approaching
Oral arguments in Trump v. Barbara — the birthright citizenship case — are approaching, with a ruling expected by end of June. Upholding Trump's EO would overturn a right guaranteed by the 14th Amendment for over a century. Catholic bishops filed an unusual amicus brief calling the order a violation of "the moral foundations of American society." Politico and the Atlantic previewed the case as the most consequential of the 2025-26 term.
Politico The Atlantic New York Times
Independent Agencies — Trump v. Slaughter Decision Pending
The Supreme Court is expected to rule on whether the president can fire commissioners of independent agencies (FTC, Federal Reserve, NLRB) without cause — potentially overturning the 1935 Humphrey's Executor precedent. The New York Times reported that justices appeared likely to side with Trump after oral arguments, with implications for the Fed's independence that alarmed financial markets.
New York Times Politico CNN
SAVE Act / Voting Rights at Supreme Court
The Supreme Court heard arguments in a case that could effectively validate provisions of Trump's SAVE Act, potentially eliminating mail-in ballot access for millions before the 2026 midterms. The Guardian and Politico previewed it as an "existential challenge to mail-in voting" arriving at a critical electoral moment. Even conservative justices appeared skeptical of the broadest arguments, though a narrow ruling could still reshape voting access.
The Guardian Politico NPR
DOJ Prosecutes Protesters on Terrorism Charges
Eight people attending a July 4th ICE protest were convicted of "material support for terrorism" for wearing black clothing — only one had discharged a weapon. The Guardian called it a "watershed moment in the criminalization of protest." A KFF analysis found that cuts affected organizations in states Trump won just as heavily as blue states — an irony that Republican strategists privately acknowledged as a midterm vulnerability.

HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. reduced the recommended childhood vaccine schedule from 17 to 11 vaccines, removing flu, rotavirus, and RSV recommendations. Major medical associations condemned the changes as politically driven and a public health risk.
