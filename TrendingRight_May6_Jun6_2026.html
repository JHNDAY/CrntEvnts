01 ICE Operations 62 mentions ▼
900,000+ Deportations — Historic Milestone
Acting ICE Director David Venturella told Breitbart News that since January 20, 2025, DHS had removed over 900,000 aliens from the United States — a pace far exceeding any prior administration. Deportees during the second week of May included convicted murderers, child sex abusers, and MS-13 gang members. The administration framed this as fulfilling Trump's core campaign mandate.
Breitbart NY Post Daily Wire
Anti-ICE Riots at NJ Delaney Hall — Violence Against Agents
Violent anti-ICE demonstrations erupted outside the ICE Delaney Hall detention facility in Newark, New Jersey, over several nights in late May. Protesters shouted death threats at federal officers — including "You're dead" and "I'll kill your whole fucking family" — and nine were arrested after biting, kicking, and punching agents. Officers ultimately deployed tear gas and pepper spray to restore order. Breitbart and the New York Post provided extensive on-the-ground coverage.
Breitbart NY Post
Democrat Officials Denounced — "North Star" Controversy
Sen. Andy Kim (D-NJ) called criminal illegal aliens detained at Delaney Hall the Democrats' "north star," a statement seized upon by Breitbart and conservative commentators as proof that Democrats had become the party of open borders. NYC Mayor Zohran Mamdani called ICE actions "cruel and inhumane" while proudly defending his sanctuary city policies — coverage widely framed on the right as Democrats choosing illegal immigrants over law-abiding citizens.
Breitbart NY Post Daily Wire
Operation Safe Drive — 114 Arrests in South Carolina
DHS and ICE announced that "Operation Safe Drive" — conducted May 12–14 on South Carolina interstates I-26 and I-85 in cooperation with state and local law enforcement — netted 114 illegal migrants. The multiday highway enforcement operation was held up by conservative media as a model of state–federal cooperation for interior enforcement.
Breitbart Daily Wire
Spokane 3 Convicted — Anti-ICE Conspirators Face Justice
A Washington State jury convicted three protesters — dubbed the "Spokane 3" by supporters — of federal conspiracy charges for attempting to prevent ICE officers from transporting two detained illegal immigrants from Spokane to Tacoma. Conservative media treated the convictions as a landmark case establishing that obstruction of immigration enforcement carries criminal consequences.
Breitbart NY Post Daily Wire
ICE Attorneys Directed to Pursue Immigration Lawyer Fraud
DHS General Counsel James Percival directed ICE attorneys to aggressively develop anti-fraud policies targeting immigration lawyers filing false asylum claims — described by right-leaning media as closing a key loophole exploited by the immigration legal industry to flood courts with manufactured cases and delay deportations indefinitely.

National Review and The Dispatch provided the most substantive right-leaning analysis of the pending birthright citizenship case (Trump v. Barbara). National Review argued the original meaning of the 14th Amendment's "subject to the jurisdiction thereof" clause did not extend citizenship to children of illegal entrants — supporting Trump's order on textualist grounds. The Dispatch offered a more cautious view, noting the 1898 Wong Kim Ark precedent made a win for Trump a longshot.

The WSJ editorial board accepted the SCOTUS ruling on IEEPA tariffs as legally correct — arguing that emergency-powers statutes shouldn't be stretched indefinitely — while criticizing the administration for relying on a legally questionable foundation in the first place. National Review ran a detailed constitutional analysis of what trade authorities remain available and their limitations.
