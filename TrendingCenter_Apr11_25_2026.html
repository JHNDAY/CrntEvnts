09 ICE Operations 23 mentions ▼
ICE Officially Targets 1 Million Deportations Per Year
ICE quietly included a formal goal of 1 million deportations per year in its congressional budget justification — confirming what had long been rumored. As of April 4, ICE had conducted 234,236 formal removals halfway through fiscal 2026. The agency deported 442,637 in the full FY2025 — far short of the million-per-year goal — though about 76% had no criminal convictions beyond immigration violations.
NYT NPR Guardian
ICE Air Deportation Flights Up 122% Year-Over-Year
ICE Flight Monitor recorded 1,794 deportation flights in March 2026 alone — a 122% increase from March 2025. ICE Air Operations used chartered flights to shuttle detainees through airports like Kansas City International, with 130 ICE Air departures from KCI in 2025 and 33 in the first quarter of 2026. Flights reached 79 countries, a 76% increase in destination diversity.
NPR Guardian CNN
287(g) Program Expands to 1,734 Agreements in 39 States
ICE had signed 1,734 memorandums of agreement with state and local law enforcement agencies under the 287(g) program as of April 23 — covering 39 states and 2 US territories. The program deputizes local officers to perform immigration enforcement functions, representing a major expansion of interior enforcement capacity beyond federal agents alone.
Politico NYT
Detention at All-Time High; Human Rights Concerns Mount
ICE detention rose by over 75% year-over-year and was on track to rival the entire federal criminal prison system by end of Trump's term. With 104 more facilities online since January 2025 — a 91% increase — advocates reported deteriorating conditions, deaths in custody, and a no-release policy that resulted in 14.3 people deported for every 1 released pending a hearing.
