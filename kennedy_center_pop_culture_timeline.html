1970s - Foundation Era
Opening with Leonard Bernstein's MASS (1971)
Jazz Folk Rock
  • 1971: Kennedy Center opens with Bernstein's MASS featuring Alvin Ailey dancers
  • 1974: Bolshoi Opera and Ballet American debuts
  • 1978: First Kennedy Center Honors established
  • 1979: Terrace Theater opens as gift from Japan
1980s - Diversification
Expanding beyond classical to embrace popular genres
Pop Jazz Broadway
  • 1986: National Symphony Orchestra becomes first artistic affiliate
  • 1987: "Shear Madness" begins continuous run (still ongoing)
  • Late 80s: International artists showcase American debuts
  • Increased programming of American musical theater and contemporary dance
1990s - Popular Integration
Embrace of contemporary American music and culture
Hip Hop R&B Contemporary
  • 1994: Dr. Billy Taylor appointed as Artistic Advisor for Jazz
  • 1997: Millennium Stage launches - free daily performances
  • Jazz-hip hop fusion programming begins
  • Contemporary dance series featuring American choreographers
  • Increased focus on cultural diversity and accessibility
2000s - Cultural Expansion
Celebrating America's multicultural musical heritage
World Music Contemporary Jazz Fusion
  • 2005: Masters of African American Choreography festival
  • 2008-2009: Modern Masters of American Dance series
  • Expansion of contemporary programming
  • Integration of technology and multimedia performances
  • Growing emphasis on American cultural diversity
2010s - Hip Hop Revolution
Formal recognition of hip hop as American art form
Hip Hop Pop Electronic
  • 2014: "One Mic: Hip-Hop Culture Worldwide" festival
  • 2015: Lady Gaga and Tony Bennett perform
  • 2016: Q-Tip appointed first Artistic Director of Hip Hop Culture
  • 2017: LL COOL J becomes first hip hop Kennedy Center Honoree
  • 2018: Hip Hop Culture Council established
2020s - Digital Innovation
Expanding reach through technology and social impact
Digital Social Impact All Genres
  • 2019: REACH campus opens - interactive arts space
  • 2020-2021: Digital programming during pandemic
  • 2023: Hip Hop 50th anniversary celebrations
  • Marc Bamuthi Joseph as VP of Social Impact
  • Artists-in-residence program featuring The Roots, Robert Glasper
  • Continued expansion of popular culture programming
