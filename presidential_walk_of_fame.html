THE PRESIDENTIAL WALK OF FAME

As Installed by President Donald J. Trump, December 2025

Location & Purpose

Location: West Wing Colonnade, connecting the White House residence to the Oval Office

Installed: Portraits unveiled September 2025; Bronze plaques added December 17, 2025

Format: Black-and-white portraits in thick gold frames with bronze plaques beneath each

Note: Many plaques were "written directly by the President himself" according to White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt

Introductory Plaque

"The Presidential Walk of Fame was conceived, built, and dedicated by President Donald J. Trump as a tribute to past Presidents, good, bad, and somewhere in the middle, who served our Country, and gave up so much in so doing."


"The Presidential Walk of Fame will long live as a testament and tribute to the Greatness of America."

Documentation Notes

Sources: Information compiled from ABC News, NBC News, CBS News, CNN, Associated Press, Newsweek, The Washington Post, and other major news outlets reporting on the Presidential Walk of Fame plaques unveiled December 17-18, 2025.


Completeness: The plaques shown here represent those with the most detailed documentation available. Some plaques for earlier presidents (George H.W. Bush, Gerald Ford, etc.) were mentioned in reports but without full text transcription.


Verification: White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt confirmed that "many were written directly by the President himself" and called them "eloquently written descriptions of each President and the legacy they left behind."


Context: This display is located along the walkway Trump uses daily to enter the Oval Office, allowing him to showcase it to visitors and foreign dignitaries.
