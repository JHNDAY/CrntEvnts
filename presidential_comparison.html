Presidential Historians Compare

Three Perspectives on Presidential Greatness • Presidents' Day 2026

Doris Kearns Goodwin Doug Brinkley Jon Meacham

Doris Kearns Goodwin

Personal Virtues

Character & Moral Qualities

"The greatest thing a president can do is the display of his character, setting an example and upholding the standards."

Key Emphasis:

  • Humility - surrounding himself with rivals
  • Empathy - reconciliation without retribution
  • Optimism - FDR changing national mood
  • Character qualities you'd want in your children
Focuses on the internal moral qualities and character traits that make presidents great role models and examples for the nation.

Doug Brinkley

Tangible Contributions

Legacy & Achievements

"Abraham Lincoln gave us foundational documents: the Gettysburg Address, the Emancipation Proclamation..."

Key Emphasis:

  • Lincoln's foundational documents
  • Washington setting institutional precedents
  • JFK's technological innovation (GPS, MRI, CAT scans)
  • Theodore Roosevelt's conservation (234M acres)
  • Carter's diplomatic achievements (Camp David)
Emphasizes concrete accomplishments and lasting institutional/environmental legacies that continue to benefit society.

Jon Meacham

Realistic Humanism

Human Complexity & Crisis Response

"We don't elect statues. We don't elect remote icons. We elect human beings."

Key Emphasis:

  • Presidents are flawed humans who rise to occasions
  • Lincoln wasn't egalitarian until the very end
  • Personal crisis response predicts leadership
  • Getting 'big things right' despite imperfections
  • Hope through human capability, not mythology
Emphasizes the humanity and imperfection of great presidents, focusing on how they responded to personal crises as predictors of national leadership.

Points of Consensus

  • ✓ Lincoln represents the pinnacle of presidential leadership
  • ✓ Character and moral qualities matter more than partisan policy
  • ✓ Theodore Roosevelt's conservation and FDR's optimism were transformative
  • ✓ Presidents should inspire hope and set moral examples

Distinct Perspectives

  • ◆ Goodwin: Internal character qualities as the measure
  • ◆ Brinkley: External achievements and lasting legacies
  • ◆ Meacham: Human imperfection and crisis response
  • ◆ All three offer complementary lenses, not competing views
