States Raising Minimum Wage (Jan 1, 2026)

On New Year's Day 2026, 19 states implemented minimum wage increases, with three additional states planning increases later in the year. This represents the largest single-day wave of state minimum wage increases.

Notable increases include:

  • Arizona: $15.15/hour (3.1% increase)
  • California: $16.90/hour
  • Colorado: $15.16/hour
  • Connecticut: $16.94/hour
  • Hawaii: $16.00/hour
  • Missouri: $15.00/hour (9% increase)
  • Nebraska: $15.00/hour (11% increase)
  • New Jersey: $15.92/hour for larger firms
  • New York: $16-17/hour depending on location
  • Virginia: $12.77/hour (2.9% increase)

Local Wage Leaders

Nearly 50 cities and localities implemented minimum wage hikes, including:

  • Tukwila, WA: $21.65/hour (highest in nation)
  • West Hollywood, CA: $20.25/hour
  • New York City: $17.00/hour

Ripple Effects

Beyond those earning minimum wage, these increases often push employers to raise salaries for workers higher up on the income ladder, creating broader economic benefits.

Early Detection Breakthroughs

GRAPE AI System (China): Capable of detecting early-stage stomach cancer through standard CT scans, a method previously considered ineffective. The system detected gastric cancer 2-10 months earlier in retrospective analysis.

Pancreatic Cancer Detection: The PANDA AI model detects pancreatic cancer through ordinary CT imaging and has been designated a breakthrough device by the FDA.

Blood-Based Detection: University of California San Diego scientists developed a test identifying 95% of early pancreatic cancers from a single drop of blood. The PAC-MANN test can detect pancreatic, ovarian, and bladder cancer at stages I and II.

Treatment Innovations

Precision Immunotherapy: AI-designed protein "keys" train immune cells to attack cancer with extreme precision. Development time reduced from years to weeks, with virtual safety screenings to avoid harmful side effects.

Personalized mRNA Vaccines: Early trials for pancreatic cancer show encouraging results. The vaccine is customized to each patient's tumor, encoding genetic instructions that train the immune system to recognize cancer-specific markers.

7-Minute Cancer Treatment: England's NHS now offers Atezolizumab injection that takes just 7 minutes to administer, compared to up to an hour for IV infusion, benefiting 3,600 patients.

AI-Powered Diagnostics

  • Mammography: Google Health's AI outperforms human experts in interpreting mammograms
  • Lung Cancer: Deep learning systems match or exceed radiologist accuracy for early-stage detection
  • Colorectal Cancer: AI automating polyp detection during colonoscopy
  • Cervical Cancer: AI achieving accuracy equal to or beyond human experts, especially valuable for regions with limited access to specialists

Improved Patient Outcomes

AI is revolutionizing cancer care by improving speed, accuracy, and consistency of detection. For pediatric cancers, the FDA approved the first therapy for recurrent diffuse midline glioma, a rare but aggressive brain cancer.

Rapid Job Growth

Clean energy jobs grew 2.8% in 2024, adding nearly 100,000 new positions. This growth rate outpaced the rest of U.S. employment (0.8%) more than three times over and far exceeded job growth in the broader energy industry.

Key Statistics:

  • Solar Jobs: 370,556 positions (60% of renewable energy generation jobs)
  • Wind Energy: 132,984 jobs, with wind turbine technicians projected to surge 60% by 2033
  • Energy Efficiency: Over 2.1 million jobs
  • EV & Battery Manufacturing: 400,000+ jobs with 20%+ year-over-year expansion
  • Clean Vehicles: 380,000 jobs in electric and hybrid vehicles

Geographic Distribution

Leading States:

  • California: 550,000+ clean energy jobs (116,380 in solar alone)
  • Texas: 260,000 jobs
  • New York: 160,000+ jobs

Fastest Growing States:

  • Montana: 15.6% year-over-year growth
  • Wyoming: 15% growth
  • Michigan, Georgia, Arizona: Rapid growth due to EV and battery manufacturing hubs

Career Opportunities

Many clean energy jobs pay well and don't require a four-year college degree:

  • Solar photovoltaic installers (one of two fastest-growing occupations)
  • Wind turbine service technicians
  • Energy storage engineers
  • Battery manufacturing technicians
  • Electric vehicle charger maintenance workers
  • Energy efficiency auditors
  • Grid integration engineers

Industry Momentum

Despite policy uncertainties, clean energy remains strong due to:

  • Solar and wind being the cheapest forms of new energy generation
  • Growing energy demands from AI and data centers
  • Tech firms like Microsoft, Google, and Amazon investing in renewable energy
  • State-level commitments (Maine: 100% clean electricity by 2040; California and Illinois passing comprehensive clean energy packages)

Future Projections

By 2034, the Bureau of Labor Statistics projects double or triple-digit employment growth in solar, wind, geothermal, and battery storage sectors. Even with federal policy changes, the industry's economic fundamentals remain strong.

Historic Improvements

The American Society of Civil Engineers' 2025 Infrastructure Report Card shows America's infrastructure is experiencing its clearest signs of improvement in decades. This reflects the early impact of the 2021 Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) and related federal programs.

IIJA Impact

The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act represents the most comprehensive federal investment in infrastructure in U.S. history:

  • Total Investment: Funding for nearly 400 infrastructure programs
  • Highway Infrastructure: Apportionments to states for FY 2022-2026
  • Electric Vehicles: National EV Infrastructure Formula Program
  • Broadband: $42.5 billion for Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment
  • Water Infrastructure: $55 billion investment, supporting 80,000 jobs annually
  • Environmental Remediation: Over 35,000 jobs related to cleanup

Job Creation & Economic Benefits

Moody's Analytics estimates significant employment impact:

  • Additional 600,000 jobs in 2024
  • Additional 800,000 jobs in 2025
  • Majority of new jobs subject to federal labor standards
  • Creates high-quality green jobs supporting wage growth

Sector Highlights

Top Performing Sectors:

  • Ports: Received highest grade (B)
  • Rail: Significant improvement
  • Airports: Major expansions and upgrades ongoing
  • Bridges: Bridge Formula Program funding

Addressing Supply Chain & Resilience

Infrastructure improvements directly address critical needs:

  • Supply Chain: $17 billion for ports and waterways; $25 billion for airports
  • Climate Resilience: $50 billion to protect against droughts and floods
  • Lead Exposure Reduction: Investing in water infrastructure to reduce health risks, particularly for Black and Hispanic Americans disproportionately affected
  • Weatherization: Home upgrades to reduce extreme weather impacts

State Leadership

Governors across the country are prioritizing infrastructure deployment:

  • Bipartisan working group established to improve federal permitting processes
  • Pennsylvania's "PA Permit Fast Track Program" streamlines critical projects
  • Focus on reducing time and cost for major infrastructure approvals
  • 65+ broadband leaders from 30 states working to close the digital divide

Looking Forward

While significant progress has been made, sustained investment is critical. ASCE estimates achieving and maintaining good repair across all infrastructure categories would require $9.1 trillion from 2024-2033. Continued federal investment at current or higher levels will be necessary for improvements to continue.

529 Plan Expansions (2025-2026)

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) expanded 529 education savings plan flexibility:

  • Expanded Uses: As of July 2025, funds can be used for credentialing, apprenticeship, and continuing education programs
  • K-12 Benefits: Starting 2026, annual K-12 withdrawal limit doubles from $10,000 to $20,000 per student
  • Tax-Free Growth: Families can use 529 funds more flexibly than before, helping limit student debt accumulation

Federal Student Aid Improvements

Despite challenges, several positive developments emerged:

  • Interest Rate Reduction Bill: Proposed legislation would lower federal student loan interest rates to 2%
  • Parent PLUS Flexibility: Proposed bills would allow Parent PLUS borrowers to repay loans under income-based plans
  • Dual Enrollment Expansion: New funding for dual enrollment and early college programs, prioritizing low-income and rural students
  • HBCU Support: Expanded allowable activities for institutions serving HBCUs and minority-serving institutions

College Affordability Initiatives

Positive Trends:

  • Net cost of college has declined on average in recent years due to steady federal investment
  • College remains a worthwhile investment: degree holders outearn non-completers by $8,000/year even after debt payments
  • Between 1991-2002, NIH-funded research contributed to 34% decrease in cancer death rates

Healthcare Access Milestone

Over 24 million consumers selected affordable health coverage through the ACA Marketplace in 2025, a record enrollment that improves healthcare access for millions of Americans.

Proposed Comprehensive Reforms

Several ambitious proposals aim to further improve affordability:

  • Debt-Free College: Federal-state partnership proposal for debt-free education at public institutions
  • Pell Grant Increases: Proposals to double Pell Grant awards over five years, with mandatory funding
  • Public Service Loan Forgiveness: Proposals to reform and reduce time to forgiveness
  • Interest Rate Caps: Proposed 5% cap on federal student loan interest rates

Support for Specific Populations

  • Arts Education: New provisions for financial aid, mentorship, and career advising for arts students at HBCUs and MSIs
  • DREAMER Students: Proposals outline federal aid eligibility pathways
  • Training Programs: Clarification of eligibility for Title IV programs exceeding minimum requirements
