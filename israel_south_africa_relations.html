Israel-South Africa Relations

A Complex History: From Alliance to Adversaries

Overview

The relationship between Israel and South Africa has undergone dramatic transformations over the past seven decades. Initially supportive following Israel's 1948 independence, relations evolved into a controversial military and nuclear alliance during South Africa's apartheid era (1967-1987), before deteriorating significantly after apartheid's end in 1994. Today, South Africa maintains only "limited political and diplomatic interaction" with Israel and has emerged as one of Israel's strongest critics, culminating in a 2023 genocide case at the International Court of Justice.

1947-1950

Birth of Israel & Early Support

Supportive Relations

South Africa voted for the 1947 UN Partition Plan and became the seventh nation to recognize Israel in May 1948. Field Marshal Jan Smuts, a Zionist supporter and friend of Chaim Weizmann, facilitated early cooperation.

Key Developments:

  • South Africa granted de facto recognition 9 days after Israeli independence
  • De jure recognition followed in May 1949
  • 120,000 South African Jews provided crucial financial support to Zionism
  • National Party government overcame earlier anti-Semitism
1950s-1960s

Israel's Criticism of Apartheid

Growing Tension

Despite growing trade, Israel consistently voted against apartheid at the UN and pursued friendships with newly independent Sub-Saharan African states.

Key Actions:

  • 1962: Israel voted for UN Resolution 1761 condemning apartheid
  • 1963: Withdrew envoy, reducing diplomatic status
  • Golda Meir declared "deep abhorrence" for racial discrimination
  • South Africa retaliated by cutting off foreign exchange flows
  • Trade continued to grow despite diplomatic distance
1967-1973

The Six-Day War Turning Point

Strategic Realignment

Israel's 1967 victory alienated African nations but won admiration from South Africa's white population. The 1973 Yom Kippur War isolated Israel further, leading to closer South African ties.

Shifting Dynamics:

  • Israel upgraded South African legation to embassy (1974)
  • South Africa reciprocated (1975)
  • Die Burger: "Israel and South Africa driven into community of interests"
  • Israel increasingly isolated from African states
  • By 1973, all but four African states severed ties with Israel
1976-1986

Military & Nuclear Alliance Peak

Deep Cooperation

The two nations formed an unprecedented military alliance, including alleged nuclear weapons collaboration. Israel became South Africa's closest military ally and primary arms supplier.

Major Collaborations:

  • 1976: PM Vorster's state visit; comprehensive cooperation pact signed
  • Israel shared highly classified Operation Opera intel with South Africa
  • Joint development of Jericho II ballistic missiles (RSA series)
  • 1979 Vela incident: Alleged joint nuclear test in Indian Ocean
  • Israel provided drone technology used in Angola (1981)
  • South Africa supplied yellowcake uranium for Israeli nuclear program
  • Israeli scientists worked at Pelindaba nuclear facility
1987

US Pressure & Sanctions

Policy Reversal

Under American pressure and threat of losing $1.8 billion in military aid, Israel announced it would not sign new military contracts with South Africa.

Changes:

  • March 1987: Shimon Peres announced end to new military contracts
  • September 1987: Ten economic and cultural sanctions imposed
  • PM P.W. Botha "surprised and outraged" by decision
  • Existing contracts allowed to continue
  • 1991: Sanctions lifted as apartheid negotiations began
1994-2000s

Post-Apartheid Deterioration

Cooling Relations

After apartheid ended, Nelson Mandela maintained diplomatic relations but was highly critical of Israeli treatment of Palestinians.

New Era:

  • Mandela: "Israel cooperated with apartheid, but did not participate in atrocities"
  • 1999: Mandela visited Israel, criticized occupation of Gaza and West Bank
  • ANC recognized both Palestinian nationalism and Israeli statehood
  • Bilateral trade peaked at $1.19 billion in 2012
  • 2012: ANC formally endorsed BDS (Boycott, Divestment, Sanctions) campaign
2018-2019

Diplomatic Downgrade

Relations Severed

South Africa withdrew its ambassador following the 2018 Gaza border protests and downgraded its Tel Aviv embassy to a liaison office.

Breaking Points:

  • May 2018: Ambassador withdrawn indefinitely
  • April 2019: Embassy downgraded to liaison office
  • ANC called Israel "an outcast and blight on humanity"
  • South Africa led campaign to strip Israel of AU observer status
  • Bilateral trade declined to $407.7 million by 2019
2023-2024

Genocide Case at ICJ

Legal Confrontation

During the 2023 Gaza war, South Africa filed a genocide case against Israel at the International Court of Justice, marking the nadir of bilateral relations.

Historic Case:

  • December 29, 2023: 84-page application filed at ICJ
  • Alleged Israel's actions "genocidal in character"
  • Requested interim order to suspend military operations
  • Israel rejected allegations "with disgust," called it "blood libel"
  • November 2023: SA Parliament voted 248-91 to close Israeli embassy
  • Israel recalled ambassador; South Africa withdrew all diplomatic staff
  • Supported by Malaysia, Turkey, and international peace groups

Current Status (2024)

Limited diplomatic interaction only. South Africa maintains that "Israel must return to negotiations and create favorable conditions for peaceful negotiations." Israel retains its Pretoria embassy, but South Africa has downgraded to a liaison office in Tel Aviv. The relationship remains defined by South Africa's sharp criticism of Israeli policy toward Palestinians and its comparison of Israel's actions to apartheid.
