Edward R. Murrow

1908-1965

The Ethical Foundation

Murrow's wartime broadcasts from London ("This... is London") established the gold standard for broadcast journalism integrity and public service.

His courage in exposing Senator Joseph McCarthy showed how broadcast journalism could serve democracy.

"We must not confuse dissent with disloyalty."

üé¨

Fred Friendly

1915-1998
Born Ferdinand Friendly Wachenheimer

The System Builder

Murrow's producer and later Ford Foundation consultant who directly shaped the creation of PBS (1969) and the public broadcasting infrastructure.

His negotiations led to the system that made NPR possible in 1970.

"Television can teach, television can illuminate... or it can simply waste the allotted time."

üîó The Bridge to NPR

Ethical DNA: Murrow's commitment to truth, objectivity, and serving democracy became NPR's foundational principles.

Infrastructure: Friendly's work at Ford Foundation directly created the Public Broadcasting Act of 1967 and Corporation for Public Broadcasting.

Legacy: NPR consistently wins Edward R. Murrow Awards, carrying forward their vision of journalism as public service.

1967
Public Broadcasting Act Passed
Influenced by Fred Friendly's Ford Foundation work, Congress creates the Corporation for Public Broadcasting, setting the legal foundation for NPR's birth three years later.
1971
All Things Considered Debuts
NPR's flagship evening news program premiered, revolutionizing radio journalism with its conversational style and comprehensive coverage. It remains America's most listened-to drive-time news program.
1979
Morning Edition Launches
NPR's morning news program begins, creating the perfect bookend to All Things Considered. Together, they would define NPR's sound and reach millions of daily commuters.
1983
Financial Crisis & Restructuring
NPR faced near-bankruptcy but emerged stronger with a new funding model emphasizing member station support and corporate underwriting, reducing dependence on federal funds.
1998
This American Life Era
The rise of narrative storytelling transforms public radio, with shows like This American Life pioneering the podcast revolution that would later explode.
2005
Podcasting Pioneer
NPR becomes an early adopter of podcasting, eventually becoming one of the most downloaded podcast publishers globally, reaching new digital-native audiences.
2008
Tiny Desk Concerts Begin
What started as a quirky office performance becomes a global phenomenon, showcasing both emerging and established artists in intimate acoustic settings, garnering billions of views.
2020
Pandemic Journalism Excellence
NPR's trusted coverage during COVID-19 leads to record listenership and digital engagement, cementing its role as America's essential news source during crisis.

Iconic Programs
All Things Considered
The most-listened-to drive-time news radio program in the US, setting the standard for thoughtful, comprehensive daily journalism since 1971.
Morning Edition
America's morning news companion for over 40 years, blending breaking news with feature reporting and cultural coverage.
Fresh Air
Terry Gross's intimate interview style has made this the gold standard for cultural journalism, featuring everyone from world leaders to indie artists.
This American Life
Ira Glass revolutionized storytelling, creating the template for modern narrative podcasting and inspiring countless imitators.
Serial
The podcast that launched the true crime phenomenon, downloaded over 340 million times and proving podcasts could achieve mainstream cultural impact.
Tiny Desk
From office concert to global cultural force, showcasing artists from Bad Bunny to T-Pain in stripped-down performances that often go viral.
