Washington D.C. Federal Incursion

After the incursion was announced, data emerged which indicated that the violent crime rate in the District had hit a 30-year low and that several cities including Houston, Memphis, Monroe LA (in Mike Johnson's CD) and Nashville had higher violent crime rates. In response, the DOJ is launching an investigation to determine if this favorable comparative data, which Trump referred to as "fake crime numbers", had been manipulated.

Carrying his 5P approach a step further, Trump "participated" in a police patrol, declared "Washington D.C. is SAFE AGAIN" and fabricated a story about a child telling him that their family had just dined out four days in a row after not having been able to do so for four years. Quite to the contrary, restaurant traffic in DC has declined and retail traffic is down 80% since the incursion.

Trump's acolytes supported the president's position as newly installed U.S. Attorney for D.C. Jeanine Pirro told prosecutors in her office to seek the highest possible charges for any federal arrests and Pete Hegseth authorized National Guard troops in the District to be armed. Joining in on her Adventures in Bad Taste, Trump will be asking Congress for $2 B to "beautify" the area within three miles of the White House and the Capitol and keep it "clean". Ukraine Peace Talks

Looking back at Trump's Ukraine peace blitz, the title of Shakespeare's play Much Ado About Nothing comes to mind. In spite of the high expectations set for last week's Alaska and White House meetings, absolutely nothing was accomplished. This was made abundantly clear by Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov on MTP yesterday when he said that an "agenda is not ready at all" for a future Putin/Zelensky meeting and questioned Zelensky's legitimacy.

It is entirely possible now that Trump will turn his attention elsewhere and reduce the U.S. support for Ukraine. In the wake of the meetings, Trump said "I've ended six wars, and I thought that maybe this would be the easiest one, and...It's a tough one". He added, referring to himself, "Anyone can make war, but only the most courageous can make peace".

Here's what some pundits had to say:

  • With regard to his ending six wars, Masha Gessen counted that he actually cited stopping seven, and then commented "That's actually seven. But also none". Later on, Trump said that he actually had ended ten wars if "you think about pre-wars".
  • Christopher Bates wrote "Trump is some combination of dishonest and delusional".
  • Bret Stephens said "By putting the interests of what Trump calls 'peace' ahead of the interests of freedom, we are all but guaranteed that Ukraine will lose both".
  • Max Bergmann of the Center for Strategic and International Studies opined "He (Trump) looks like someone trying to salvage his credibility but is unwilling to get tough with Putin and back Ukraine".
  • With regard to assertions that progress had been made toward an agreement which could soon be reached, Celeste Wallander who served in the Biden DOD said "Russia and Ukraine aren't just miles apart, they are hundreds of miles apart".
  • Jennifer Rubin commented "Ukraine has found out the hard way that U.S. security guarantees are worthless...and when offered under this president (are) more likely a recipe for extortion".

Elections

In TX, the state legislature passed the congressional gerrymander bill which Trump requested to gain 3-5 House seats and Governor Abbott said "One Big Beautiful Map has passed the Senate, and is on the way to my desk, where it will quickly be signed into law". A Democratic state representative commented that it only took 445k white residents to secure a congressional seat under the new map compared to 1.4 MM Latinos and 2.0 MM blacks.

Under Governor Newsom's leadership, CA responded in kind and passed a redistricting bill, contingent upon TX following through, which would defer the authority of its independent election commission until after the 2030 census. This will require a special election in November to suspend the state's current congressional map. Commenting on this CA approach, Democratic strategist Jamaal Simmons said "Democrats are over being the 'nice guy' party". It should be noted that former governor Schwarzenegger opposes this ballot measure and no credible polling data on public support is yet available.

Trump is calling for gerrymanders without justification in all states where Republicans have full control as he is deploying his favored "might makes right" approach. Given that Republicans hold the trifecta in 23 states versus 15 for the Democrats, they have a considerable advantage in this regard. Turning Republican gerrymandering on its head, Speaker Mike Johnson brazenly said "Democrats across the nation have played politics with redistricting for decades".
