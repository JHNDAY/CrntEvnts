The Standoff — Two Immovable Positions
The Government
The Pentagon
🎖 A.I. must be available for all lawful purposes. A contractor cannot limit how national security tools are used.
🚀 Wants to aggressively integrate A.I. in war planning and weapons development under Sec. Hegseth's mandate.
⚖️ "Just as a weapons manufacturer does not determine where its missiles are dropped."
THREAT: Defense Production Act · Supply Chain Designation
vs.
The Company
Anthropic
🛡 Cannot in good conscience allow Claude to enable mass domestic surveillance of Americans.
🤖 Refuses to permit use in fully autonomous weapons with no humans in the decision loop.
💬 CEO Dario Amodei: "We are prepared to lose this contract and help DOD transition to another provider."

How We Got Here
November 2024
The Ukraine war accelerates autonomous drone warfare, shifting A.I. in weapons from theory to battlefield reality.
January 20, 2025
Trump revokes Biden-era A.I. safety executive orders. A.I. regulation becomes a secondary priority across Washington.
January 9, 2026
Sec. Hegseth publishes memo calling for A.I. companies to remove all restrictions from their technologies. Anthropic is singled out.
February 26–27, 2026
Pentagon and Anthropic go public. Hegseth threatens the Defense Production Act. Amodei refuses. Google and OpenAI employees revolt.
