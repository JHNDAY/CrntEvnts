Jan – Mar 2025
IEEPA Tariffs Begin
Trump invokes the 1977 International Emergency Economic Powers Act — never before used for tariffs — to impose duties on China, Canada, and Mexico citing fentanyl crisis and trade deficits.
10–20% on China · Canada · Mexico
April 2, 2025
"Liberation Day" — Global Tariffs
Trump announces sweeping "reciprocal tariffs" on virtually every trading partner. Minimum 10% on all imports; dozens of nations face much higher rates. Markets worldwide react sharply.
10%+ on ALL countries
May 28, 2025
International Trade Court Rules: Illegal
A unanimous three-judge panel at the U.S. International Court of Trade rules IEEPA does not authorize tariffs. Tariffs remain in effect pending appeal — government collects anyway.
Aug 29, 2025
Appeals Court Upholds: Still Illegal
The U.S. Court of Appeals affirms the lower court. Tariffs continue in effect. Nearly 2,000 importers file cases seeking refunds — all stayed pending Supreme Court.
Nov 5, 2025
Supreme Court Oral Arguments
The Court hears consolidated cases Learning Resources v. Trump and V.O.S. Selections v. United States. Key questions: Does IEEPA include tariff power? Does the Major Questions Doctrine apply?
Feb 20, 2026 — THE RULING
Supreme Court: 6-3 — IEEPA Cannot Authorize Tariffs
Chief Justice Roberts, joined by Gorsuch, Barrett, Sotomayor, Kagan & Jackson: "Those words cannot bear such weight." All IEEPA tariffs invalidated. $160B+ collected illegally. Refund question sent back to lower courts.
Feb 20, 2026 — Hours Later
Trump: "Shameful" — Signs New 10% Global Tariff
Trump calls ruling "deeply disappointing," attacks Gorsuch and Barrett as "an embarrassment to their families." Same afternoon signs executive order using Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 — a different law — for 10% global tariff.
New: 10% → 15% global
Feb 21–25, 2026
What Comes Next: Uncertainty Continues
Trump raises new tariff to 15%. FedEx sues for refunds. EU, China, Germany reassess leverage. Section 301 and 338 investigations launched. Treasury says revenue impact "virtually unchanged." Legal battles over $160B+ in refunds begin.
