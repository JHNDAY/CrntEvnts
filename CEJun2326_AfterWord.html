01 Iran Deal at Versailles: $300B Fund Disputed, Israel Breaks Ranks High coverage ▼
Signed at Versailles, During a G7 Dinner
Trump and Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian signed a 14-point memorandum of understanding on June 17 at the Palace of Versailles, during a dinner hosted by Emmanuel Macron at the G7 summit. The deal sets a 60-day window to negotiate a permanent treaty, reopens the Strait of Hormuz, and begins lifting sanctions.
NBC NewsTimes of Israel
The $300B Fund — Trump Denies It Publicly
Press accounts diverge sharply on the reconstruction fund: several outlets describe a $300 billion fund tied to a future permanent treaty, while Trump told reporters at the G7 that reports of a $300 billion U.S. investment were a "false story" and that Washington was "not investing 10 cents." The discrepancy remains unresolved in subsequent coverage.
Fox NewsCultura Colectiva
Netanyahu: Israel Isn't Bound, Lebanon Operations Continue
Israel was not a signatory to the memorandum. Netanyahu said publicly that Israel does not consider itself bound by the ceasefire terms covering Lebanon and would continue military operations there — a structural gap in an agreement that explicitly lists Lebanon as a front where hostilities must end.

02 Israel's New Death-Penalty Laws: 96% Conviction Rate, Show Trials, Champagne High coverage ▼
March Law: Death by Hanging, Palestinians Only
The Knesset passed the Death Penalty for Terrorists Law 62–47 on March 30, mandating death by hanging as the default sentence for West Bank Palestinians convicted of deadly attacks in military courts, with sentencing carried out within 90 days. A separate provision technically applies to anyone "denying Israel's right to exist," but is written to exclude Jewish defendants.
Times of IsraelDPIC
May Law: A Tribunal for October 7th
On May 11, the Knesset voted 93–0 to create a special military tribunal — 15 judges, a dedicated Jerusalem courtroom, public livestreamed trials — to try roughly 300 detained Palestinians on charges up to genocide for the October 7 attacks. Estimated cost: between $689 million and $1.72 billion, depending on which ministry's figures are used.
Al JazeeraTimes of Israel
Rights Groups: Discriminatory, Unconstitutional, Apartheid
Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, and Israel's own Association for Civil Rights petitioned against the March law as discriminatory by design; UN experts and the EU separately warned it violates the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights. Israel's High Court has yet to issue a final ruling on the pending challenges.
Amnesty InternationalDPIC
Ben-Gvir's Champagne, and a Photographer Swept In
National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir, whose Otzma Yehudit party authored the March bill, celebrated its passage in the Knesset chamber; party members had worn noose-shaped lapel pins during the debate. Among those now facing the May tribunal's jurisdiction is at least one detainee whose presence on October 7 was reportedly as a photographer.

03 Trump Booed at the Garden, Three Ex-Presidents Toast Obama's Library High coverage ▼
Thunderous Boos at Game 3, Not the Title Itself
Trump was loudly booed when shown on the jumbotron during the anthem at NBA Finals Game 3 on June 8 — a game the Knicks lost, 115–111, falling to a 2–1 series lead. He watched from a bulletproof-glass suite with owner James Dolan; a planned outdoor fan watch party was canceled for security reasons. The Knicks went on to win the title in five games — their first since 1973.
CNBCAl Jazeera
"He's Not Wanted Here" — Local Reaction
Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer and Rep. Hakeem Jeffries both criticized Trump's attendance as self-insertion into a city moment; NBA Commissioner Adam Silver said Trump was "welcome to be here." Fact-checkers confirmed the boos were genuine and synced to his image appearing on screen, not a fabricated clip.
Al Jazeera
Obama Center Opens — Three Other Living Ex-Presidents Attend
The Obama Presidential Center's June 18 dedication in Chicago drew Bill Clinton, George W. Bush, and Joe Biden — all three other living former presidents — plus their spouses. Trump, the sitting president, did not attend. Obama's speech invoked "no kings," a popular anti-Trump protest phrase, while also calling for national healing.

04 Massachusetts: Rent Control Killed Same-Day, Open-Primary Question Survives Developing story ▼
Struck Down the Morning of the Session
The SJC ruled the rent-control ballot question unconstitutional on the morning of June 23 — the same day BOLLI met — because its exemption for "educational, religious, or non-profit" housing made religion "a factor in its application," which the state Constitution bars from initiative petitions. The proposal would have capped annual rent increases at 5% or inflation, whichever is lower.
WBURAxios Boston
A Separate Tax Question Fell the Week Before
A proposed income-tax-rebate ballot question was blocked the week prior on different grounds — the SJC found its official summary misleading, not religion-related. The two rulings were separate decisions roughly a week apart, not a single same-day double-strike.
CommonWealth Beacon
The Open-Primary Question Survives Court Review
In a separate ruling, the same court let stand a petition to replace party primaries with a single all-party primary for state offices, top-two advancing to November — the "jungle primary" model BOLLI discussed. It will appear on the November 2026 ballot alongside a marijuana-related measure.

06 DOJ Hollowed Out: The "Broadview Six" Case and an 0-for-9 Run on Voter Rolls Sustained coverage ▼
Redacted Transcripts, "Trust Has Been Broken"
Federal Judge April Perry dismissed all remaining charges against the "Broadview Six" Chicago protesters after discovering prosecutors had submitted redacted grand jury transcripts to conceal improper conduct, including coaching jurors and dismissing one who called the case a "crock of sh*t." Perry told the U.S. Attorney directly that the "presumption of regularity" — courts' default trust in DOJ representations — "has been broken."
Law&CrimeCBS News
NYT: A Pattern, Not an Anomaly
The New York Times' Alan Feuer reported that politicized prosecutions are producing an "erosion in faith" among both grand jurors and judges, citing repeated grand jury rejections in the Comey and Letitia James cases and a Wyoming panel that threw out nine indictments after a prosecutor told jurors they'd hear about "bad guys" and "murderers" before any evidence was presented.
NYT Politics
0-for-9 on Voter Roll Lawsuits
DOJ has filed 31 lawsuits against states refusing to hand over unredacted voter rolls; nine have been decided, and the department has lost every one — including before judges spanning the spectrum, five appointed by Trump and four by Democratic presidents. No federal court has yet ruled in DOJ's favor.
Democracy DocketBrennan Center
111 Former Prosecutors Demand an Investigation
More than 100 former federal prosecutors from the Northern District of Illinois, along with House Judiciary Ranking Member Jamie Raskin, have called for a formal investigation into U.S. Attorney Andrew Boutros over the Broadview Six conduct, which they describe as a documented "failure of leadership."
