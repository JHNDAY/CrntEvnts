Appeals courts — with more Trump appointees — have been more favorable to the administration.

Why Winning in Court May Not Be Enough

Even when the administration loses, the damage is often done. Researchers halt studies. Nonprofits lay off staff. Universities rethink mission statements.

Each injunction covers only the specific parties and programs in that suit — leaving the administration free to withhold funds from others not in court.

Legal scholars describe it as a "three-card monte" strategy: lose one case, pivot to the next category of targets.

The Constitutional Stakes
  • ▸Separation of Powers — Can the president override Congress's "power of the purse"?
  • ▸Due Process (5th Amendment) — Do grantees have a right to be heard before funds are cut?
  • ▸1st Amendment — Can the government punish advocacy by cutting grants?
  • ▸Equal Protection (5th Amendment) — Is singling out states by partisan lean unconstitutional? (First ruling: yes — St. Paul v. Wright)
