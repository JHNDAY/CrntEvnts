This Slap in the Face to Rural America is a Chance to Turn it Blue

By Andy Beshear, Governor of Kentucky and Incoming Chair of the Democratic Governors Association
November 24, 2025 | 5 min read
Core Thesis: Trump's policies are devastating rural America, giving Democrats an opening to win back these communities by becoming the party of aspiration, speaking plainly, and explaining their values.
1. Democrats Must Be the Party of Aspiration ▼

The Core Problem

The widespread belief that the American Dream is unattainable or dead. This dream — that if you work hard and play by the rules, you can get ahead — has inspired generations.

Beyond Affordability

While tackling affordability is important, Democrats need to offer more. Families need to believe they can:

  • Build a better life, not just make ends meet
  • Set their families up for long-term success
  • Take vacations that are now slipping out of reach
  • Achieve goals beyond day-to-day survival

The Democratic Vision

Democrats should be the party that makes it possible to build a better life — one focused on aspiration and upward mobility, not just survival.

2. Trump's Policies Are Devastating Rural Communities ▼

The "One Big Ugly Bill"

17 million Americans will be kicked off healthcare (including 200,000 Kentuckians)

35 rural hospitals threatened in Kentucky alone; 338 nationwide

20,000 healthcare jobs at risk in Kentucky

Direct Rural Impacts

  • Rural maternal health centers forced to close: Women will have to drive hours to give birth
  • Lost workforce productivity: People must travel for appointments, losing a day's work
  • Economic ripple effects: Coffee shops, local banks, insurance agencies all take a hit
  • Tariffs jacking up prices: Across the board, for no reason

Government Shutdown Cruelty

Trump was willing to use the hunger of Americans — including children and seniors — as a bargaining chip. It was cruel, wrong, and importantly, it backfired.

3. Recent Democratic Victories Show the Path Forward ▼

November 2025 Wins

  • Statewide victories in Georgia
  • Held the Supreme Court in Pennsylvania
  • Broke the supermajority in Mississippi State Senate
  • Two gubernatorial elections won by double-digit margins
  • New Jersey (Mikie Sherrill) and Virginia (Abigail Spanberger) won by focusing on pocketbook pressures

What These Victories Showed

A direct repudiation of the Trump agenda — but Democrats still need to grapple with lost ground in many parts of the country, especially rural America.

4. Talk Like Normal Human Beings ▼

Stop Using Bureaucratic Language

Democrats won't win the messaging battle with euphemisms. Use plain, honest language:

Don't say: "food insecure"
Say: "hungry"
Don't say: "substance use disorder"
Say: "addiction"

Why Plain Language Matters

Beshear lost friends and acquaintances to addiction. "Addiction is hard, it's mean, and it kills people. So when people triumph over it, we should give them the credit they deserve by calling it what it is."

5. Communicate Your "Why" — Not Just Your "What" ▼

Democrats Are Good at the "What"

But need to get better at explaining the "why" — the values and beliefs that drive their policies.

Beshear's LGBTQ Veto Example

What he did: Vetoed the "nastiest piece of anti-LGBTQ legislation in the country"

His "why": His faith. He believes all children are children of God and didn't think the legislature should be picking on vulnerable kids.

Result: Despite tens of millions of dollars in misleading attack ads, he won because he gave Kentuckians the respect of explaining his veto and his reasoning.

For Beshear, It's Faith

For other Democrats, the "why" might be different, but explaining the deeper values behind policy positions connects with voters and builds trust.

6. Concrete Example: Henderson County Success Story ▼

The Numbers Tell the Story

2019: Beshear narrowly won Henderson County

2023: Won Henderson County by double digits

What Changed?

The Project: Opened the cleanest, greenest recycled paper mill

Jobs Created: 320

Starting Pay: Almost $40/hour including benefits

Location: A former coal town that had felt forgotten

The Impact

"That paper mill resurrected the American Dream for 320 families."

This is how Democrats can win back areas that have been slipping away — by delivering tangible economic improvements that restore hope and opportunity.

7. The Path to Winning Back America ▼

Three Core Commitments

  • Address day-to-day concerns: Show American families that Democrats are committed to their immediate needs
  • Believe in a brighter future: Offer genuine hope for their children's prospects
  • Always give straight talk: Speak honestly and plainly, without euphemisms or bureaucratic language

The Hard Work Required

Democrats must do the work of convincing families that the party will do what Donald Trump and the Republicans have shown they will not.

The Opportunity

Trump has given Democrats a huge opening by making it harder for people to even get by. Democrats must seize this moment with urgency, focus, and a clear, tangible vision.
