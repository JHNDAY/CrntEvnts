Tom Lehrer

Mathematical Satirist & Musical Genius

Early Life

Born April 9, 1928, in New York City. Child prodigy who entered Harvard at 15, graduating with a mathematics degree. Pursued graduate studies while developing his satirical musical talents.

Creative Peak (1953-1972)

Released groundbreaking albums of satirical songs that skewered politics, social issues, and human folly with mathematical precision and devastating wit.

  • "Songs by Tom Lehrer" (1953)
  • "More of Tom Lehrer" (1959)
  • "That Was the Year That Was" (1965)

The Great Silence

After 1972, virtually stopped performing and recording. Returned to academia full-time, teaching mathematics and later music theater at UC Santa Cruz until retirement.

J.D. Salinger

Literary Recluse & Voice of a Generation

Early Life

Born January 1, 1919, in New York City. Served in WWII, experiencing combat that would profoundly influence his writing. Began publishing short stories in the 1940s.

Creative Peak (1948-1965)

Produced works that defined post-war American literature, capturing teenage alienation and spiritual searching with unprecedented authenticity.

  • "The Catcher in the Rye" (1951)
  • "Nine Stories" (1953)
  • "Franny and Zooey" (1961)
  • "Raise High the Roof Beam, Carpenters" (1963)

The Great Withdrawal

After 1965, published virtually nothing. Retreated to Cornish, New Hampshire, living as a recluse, fiercely guarding his privacy until his death in 2010.
