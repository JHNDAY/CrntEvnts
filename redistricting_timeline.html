August 3, 2025
Texas Democrats Flee State
More than 50 Texas House Democrats left the state to deny Republicans a quorum needed to vote on a redistricting plan that would create five new GOP congressional seats at President Trump's request. Most flew to Chicago, with others going to New York and Massachusetts.

August 4-5, 2025
Texas Issues Arrest Warrants
The Texas House voted 85-6 to issue civil arrest warrants for the absent Democrats. Governor Abbott threatened to remove them from office and directed Texas Rangers to investigate potential bribery charges. The warrants were largely symbolic as they couldn't be enforced outside Texas.
Sources: Texas Tribune - Texas House issues arrest warrants for Democrats who left state CNN - Texas Republicans push ahead with redistricting after Democrats flee state
August 8, 2025
California Signals Response
Governor Gavin Newsom hosted Texas Democratic lawmakers and announced California would respond with its own redistricting to "nullify" Texas's gains. New York Governor Kathy Hochul and Illinois Governor JB Pritzker also hosted Texas Democrats and signaled potential responses.

August 15, 2025
California Unveils Counter-Map
California Democrats released their redistricting proposal aimed at producing five additional Democratic seats to directly counter Texas's five Republican seats. The plan would go to voters in a November special election, bypassing California's independent redistricting commission.
Sources: Washington Post - Democrats redraw House maps in California, countering Texas Republicans NPR - California Democrats unveil congressional map to counter Trump
August 18, 2025
Strategic Coordination Complete
After California had finalized its counter-strategy, Texas Democrats returned home. The timing was explicitly coordinated - reports noted that "following California's gerrymandering proposal to counter Republican gains in Texas, most of the Texas Democrats returned to the state."
Sources: Wikipedia - 2025 Texas proposed redistricting NPR - Texas Democrats end two-week walkout over redistricting
August 21, 2025
Texas Passes Redistricting
With Democrats back and quorum restored, the Texas Legislature passed the redistricting plan creating five new Republican-leaning congressional seats. The outcome was the same as if Democrats had never left, but the strategic coordination with other states was complete.
Sources: NPR - Texas passes midterm redistricting sought by Trump as California plans to counter CNN - California Democrats approve Newsom's redistricting plan after Texas House passes GOP-drawn maps
August 22, 2025
California Finalizes Response
California lawmakers passed the final redistricting package, setting up a November 4, 2025 special election where voters will decide whether to adopt the new map that would create five Democratic seats to offset Texas's five Republican seats.
