Why We Need a New Name for Our Era

The Cold War ended. The Post-Cold War era has ended. We've entered something entirely new: a moment where accelerating climate change, rapid technological transformation, shifting geopolitics, and unprecedented connectivity are fusing together to create an explosion of complexity across every domain of human life.

POLYCENE

From the Greek "poly" meaning "many" ‚Äî an epoch where every person and machine increasingly has a voice to be heard and a lever to impact one another, and the planet, at previously unimaginable speed and scale. Binary systems are giving way to multiple interconnected ones across all domains.

The holy grail: creating machines capable of "polymathic artificial general intelligence" ‚Äî mastering physics, chemistry, biology, computer science, philosophy, Mozart, Shakespeare, and baseball better than any human, then reasoning across all disciplines at higher dimensions to produce breakthrough insights no human ever could.

The Computing Phase Change:

  • From: Programmable computing (machines reflect human programmer's insight)
  • To: Polymathic AGI (you describe the outcome, AI figures out the rest)
  • Enabled by: Chips evolving from binary to parallel processing

Climate change is no longer just "warming bad, cooling good." It's become the spark that ignites cascades of interlocking crises. Self-reinforcing events like melting ice caps and Amazon destruction propel us toward higher temperatures, triggering droughts, floods, wildfires, crop failures, and sea-level rise ‚Äî which unleash economic shocks, mass migration, state collapse, and breakdown of trust worldwide.

The Cascade Effect:

  • Weakening resilience of natural systems
  • Greater connectivity means crises cross borders instantly
  • Example: Syrian drought ‚Üí rural migration ‚Üí urban pressure ‚Üí food prices ‚Üí civil war

üåêGeopolitics Goes Polyamorous

The world is no longer defined by binaries (East-West, Communist-Capitalist, North-South). It's become a free-for-all square dance of shifting partners. Middle powers practice "multialignment" ‚Äî seeking advantage issue by issue rather than binding to one camp.

Examples of New Alignments:

  • Iran aligned with Russia against Ukraine
  • China supplying drone technology to both Russia and Ukraine
  • Israel aligned with Muslim Azerbaijan versus Christian Armenia
  • India buying Russian oil while courting Western investment

üè¢From Binary Trade to Poly-Economic Networks

Adam Smith's bilateral trade (I make cheese, you make wine) has evolved into global ecosystems. Today's economy operates inside interdependent webs of knowledge, skills, technology, and trust. Most trade involves more than two countries, with about 70% of international trade now through global supply chains.

The Division of Knowledge:

  • Products designed in one country, sourced from multiple others
  • Manufactured in one place, assembled in another, tested elsewhere
  • Example: Smartphone chips imagined in California, designed with US/European software, manufactured in Taiwan with Dutch machines and Japanese materials, assembled in China

üë•Communities Become Polymorphic

The social landscape has transformed from rigid binaries to diverse mosaics. Communities once defined by single ethnicity or faith are now polyglot, polychromatic, and polyreligious. Global migration has roughly doubled since 1990, becoming multidirectional across all continents.

Example: St. Louis Park, Minnesota

  • 1950s: Binary (white/Black, Christian/Jewish, clear boundaries)
  • Today: 29-year-old Somali Muslim woman as mayor
  • 30+ languages spoken in elementary schools
  • Representative: Ilhan Omar, one of first Muslim women in Congress

How to Govern in the Polycene

Most problems we face no longer have "either/or" answers ‚Äî they have "both/and" answers. The best solutions live in synthesis, not on the edges. We need high walls with big gates, more police and better police, free trade with reciprocal rules, diplomacy backed by strength.

The most adaptive communities will assemble dynamic coalitions across business, labor, government, social entrepreneurs, philanthropists, innovators, regulators, and educators to solve problems through synthesis rather than postponing them with binary mutual vetoes.

"Interdependence is no longer our choice. It is our condition. We will either build healthy interdependencies and rise together or suffer through unhealthy interdependencies and fall together."

The Polycene will be the first era in which humanity must govern, innovate, collaborate, and coexist at a planetary scale in order to thrive. It will take everyone, everywhere, rowing together.
