The Iran War: Theatrics vs. Substance

Comic Takes on the Conflict

  • SNL's Colin Jost on Iran holding no cards: "They are literally holding a straight" — a poker-hand pun well received by the group
  • Jimmy Kimmel: Trump "talks about war like he's bragging to Billy Bush about women"
  • John noted 71 Iranian negotiators faced essentially Vance, Witkoff, and Kushner for the U.S.
  • By contrast, the JCPOA took 20 months to negotiate

IMF Warning

Global Outlook "Abruptly Darkened"

The IMF chief economist stated the global outlook has abruptly interrupted what had been a steady growth trajectory — attributing the deterioration solely to the war.

John's Core Analysis: Theatrics as Strategy

The Pattern

Trump's pattern: say something outrageous (e.g., threatening to "destroy Iran with terrible weapons"), generate press hysteria, then quietly back off. The Straits remain closed, annihilation is off the table, and the talks collapsed. "He's consistently playing the room for dramatic effect… he's the most unserious person."
Francesca's Counterpoint (Peter Baker / NYT): A recent Times article questions whether Trump's statements reflect strategic theater or genuine cognitive decline — noting observers across the political spectrum are wondering if he "doesn't have a filter anymore, doesn't have a good grip on reality."
John's Response: Roy Cohn's maxim — "no news is bad news, just stay in the news cycle" — has governed Trump's behavior for decades. Whether demented or theatrical, the effect is the same: permanent chaos.
George: "He didn't achieve what he set out to achieve. The straits are still closed and we're not talking about annihilation anymore." The negotiations lasted roughly 21 hours — "almost 24."

Hungary: "Magyar Defeated MAGA"

The Election Result

A Decisive Rebuke of Orbanism

  • Peter Magyar's coalition won 138 seats; Orbán retained only 55 (plus 6 to a far-right party)
  • Magyar won 70% of parliamentary seats on 77% voter turnout
  • Magyar, 45, is pro-EU, pro-democracy, anti-Putin — described as "explicitly anti-MAGA"
  • Magyar hopes to take over as early as May 5th; asked Orbán to cooperate in the transition

The MAGA Connection

Heritage Foundation's Endorsement, Now Embarrassing

Kevin Roberts of the Heritage Foundation called Hungary "not just a model for conservative statecraft, but THE model." MAGA operatives traveled to Budapest "like a pilgrimage site." The election result landed, per Kimmel's commentary, "like a slap across the face of Trump world's entire worldview."

Jimmy Kimmel Commentary (Played in Session)

On the deeper lesson for America: "The most dangerous lie in modern politics is inevitability — because it's the lie that keeps citizens home. High turnout is what breaks that spell." On authoritarians: "They depend on inevitability. They want people to believe resistance is futile." On MAGA's real fascination: "Not freedom in any meaningful civic sense… MAGA's fascination is with domination — how to win without earning consent."

John's Assessment

A Sign of Hope

Hungary had to endure 16 years of illiberal governance. But through democratic processes, they finally said "enough." The lesson for America: "6 months ago there was a sense of gloom that it's all over, Trump's gonna do whatever he wants. That's what he wanted us to think. But in fact that's not true."
Wry Note: "So Maggar has defeated Maga for the moment. And Leo XIV is doing quite a good job, too."

Congressional Resignations: Swalwell & Gonzalez

The Double Departure

  • Eric Swalwell resigned from Congress after dropping out of the California governor's race
  • Tony Gonzalez (R-TX) resigned shortly after — John had suspected dirty tricks, since Gonzalez had served six terms (since 2014) without scandal until he entered the governor's race
  • Gonzalez admitted errors in judgment, including sending explicit photos and two documented affairs; one romantic interest burned herself to death
  • George Santos was the 6th expulsion from Congress; this deal prevented a 7th

The Jeffries-Johnson Deal

A Quiet Bipartisan Bargain

Rather than take the Gonzalez case to a floor vote for expulsion (which had only happened 6 times in history), Hakeem Jeffries and Mike Johnson struck a deal: each party loses one member, the balance of power stays intact (Republicans hold a slim 2-vote majority), and both sides avoid a messy public spectacle.

California Governor's Race Update

New Landscape: With Swalwell out and Trump endorsing TV personality Steve Hilton (which George sees as damaging to the other Republican, Sheriff Bianco), it is now "quite likely one of the Democrats will go through." John personally liked Swalwell but acknowledged his departure opens the field.

Trump's Mental State & the 25th Amendment

A Serious Story Emerges

Cross-Partisan Concern

A news story over the weekend described figures from the right, center, and left — including some of Trump's own allies — discussing the 25th Amendment in what John called "a somewhat serious way," driven by alarm at his "absolute over-the-top, impulsive, borderline demonic" behavior.
Francesca (citing Peter Baker, NYT): Baker's article questioned whether Trump is strategically manipulating or genuinely losing his grip on reality — noting observers across the spectrum, even former supporters, are raising this question.

Why It Won't Happen

  • The 25th Amendment requires cabinet action — "and the cabinet are all sycophants"
  • Once Trump declares himself recovered, the process reverses — "it's designed more for someone having surgery"
  • J.D. Vance "won't be a profile in courage"
  • Impeachment also off the table: "If it didn't happen after January 6th, it won't happen now"
  • The value of the story: the fact that it was discussed at all "really spoke to the severity of him crossing the line, even for him"
John on Roy Cohn's Legacy: Trump has lived by the maxim "no news is bad news — just stay in the news cycle." Whether it's pathology or strategy, the behavioral result is identical.
Batting Average Anecdote: Trump claimed to be "the best baseball player in New York State." Someone located his actual statistics — a .047 batting average. "Maybe he's always been crazy."

The Board of Peace: Gaza Governance Update

What Was Approved vs. What Is Planned

Key Distinction: UN Security Council Resolution 2803 approved the Board of Peace as applying to Gaza — but the Board's own mission statement covers any area of conflict, not just Gaza. The gap between what was approved and what's envisioned is significant.

Structure of the Board

  • 3 voting member states, 25 non-voting members; 3-year terms (or permanent for a $1B payment — nobody has paid)
  • 19 countries have signed on; none from Western Europe
  • Supposed to receive $17B in funding; only $1B received
  • Executive Board: chaired by Vlad Anovov (Bulgaria), with Jared Kushner, Witkoff, and Tony Blair — no Palestinians
  • Trump is "king for life" — only he can dissolve the Board; no succession plan

Kushner's Gaza Plan

Luxury Redevelopment, Minimal Palestinian Housing

  • Plans for luxury high-rises, resort hotels, Israeli residential units — "Dubai 2"
  • Only 100,000 housing units for Palestinians — sufficient for ~500,000 people
  • No provision for the remaining 1.5M+ Gazans: "They're just supposed to go away. They don't want to go, and nobody wants them."
  • No recognition of prior land ownership — deeds will not be honored
  • Palestinians who are screened may live in barracks and work for the governing body — paid in scrip. John: "Sort of like the company store."

Territorial Situation

  • At ceasefire, Israel occupied ~52% of Gaza, divided by a "yellow line" (yellow barrels at intervals)
  • The yellow line has since moved west — Israel now occupies 58% of the strip
  • An International Stabilization Force is planned to dismantle tunnels; a 15-member National Committee (the "Technical Committee") would administer Gaza
John's Assessment: "It's not gonna happen. Where are they gonna take these million and a half people?"

Military Dissent: A Farewell to Arms

The Hemingway Frame

John's Organizing Metaphor: Inspired by an NPR article on growing dissent within the military, John drew a parallel to A Farewell to Arms — where Lieutenant Henry walks away from the military when its demands cross a moral line. "Is this the beginning of people walking away from the military?"

The Military's Tradition of Nonpartisanship

  • Military personnel take an oath to the Constitution — and "tend to take that seriously"
  • The military leans right, but is a professional, historically non-political institution
  • Five of the six recently departed members of Congress who spoke out were military veterans; the other two were CIA
  • Hegseth — low-level officer, now Secretary of Defense — "I'm sure the professionals have no regard for that guy"
  • Top generals summoned to a meeting "sat there stone-faced." Those were the real deal, career professionals.

Hegseth's Record

A Secretary the Military Does Not Respect

  • His main qualification: TV presence and loyalty to Trump
  • Fired generals — including women and Black officers — off promotion lists
  • Kicked out transgender service members (who are still receiving salaries)
  • Converted the office next to his into a makeup room
  • In 2016 he said soldiers don't have to follow illegal orders — "of course now it's totally different"

Judiciary vs. Military: A Comparison

Two Institutions, Different Guardrails

John compared the two major institutional counterweights to Trump's power. The judiciary has held — courts have issued strong rulings, putting real brakes on the administration. But the military is asymmetric: Hegseth has been firing people "willy-nilly," with no equivalent to judicial tenure protections. Immigration judges (executive branch, not judiciary) have been especially vulnerable — 113 fired out of 730, despite an already inadequate supply.
  • Retention is declining — people are not re-enlisting because they don't like what they see
  • Concern about orders crossing constitutional lines (e.g., shelling a vessel without knowing who was aboard)
  • Using military as domestic police force: "the next step to an authoritarian state"
  • Courts can hold officials in contempt — but the marshals who enforce contempt orders work for the executive branch. The structural loop is incomplete.
