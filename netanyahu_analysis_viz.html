Substantial and compelling

The evidence strongly suggests that while Israel's national security concerns are genuine and powerful drivers of the Gaza war, Netanyahu's policy to continue the conflict is not entirely unconnected to his desire to avoid prosecution and maintain political power.

The confluence of ongoing domestic corruption trials, unprecedented international war crimes warrants, a precarious governing coalition, and explicit accusations from diverse stakeholders indicates a significant and complex interplay of motivations.

This dynamic potentially hinders genuine ceasefire efforts, exacerbates humanitarian crises, and raises critical questions about democratic accountability, judicial independence, and the long-term stability of Israel's political system.
