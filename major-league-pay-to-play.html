The Criminal Case

■ Breaking · Federal Court, Brooklyn · March 14–15, 2026
The Joshua Nass Arrest
A Trump-world lawyer charged with extortion — after allegedly hiring muscle to collect $500K owed for pardon-brokering
Who Is He
Joshua Nass, 34 — lawyer & lobbyist, website bills him as "well-connected" and specializing in "executive branch advocacy." Photos on social media alongside Don Trump Jr. at Mar-a-Lago.
The Charge
Arrested March 14, 2026. Accused of hiring someone to do "whatever it takes" — including physically dragging the client's son from his home — to extort the $500K debt owed for pardon services.
The Client (John Doe 1)
Believed to be Joseph Schwartz — convicted for $38M nursing home tax fraud. Trump pardoned him Nov. 14, 2025. Schwartz had earlier paid Wohl & Burkman $960K for the same goal, then switched to Nass.
What Nass Filed
In January 2026, Nass disclosed receiving $100K in lobbying fees (Nov–Dec 2025) for "advocacy efforts regarding executive clemency" — the paper trail that began unraveling the case.

The Price of Freedom

How the Pardon Market is Priced — Per the WSJ, NBC & NYT

$960K
Jack Burkman & Jacob Wohl — Nursing Home Fraud
Convicted felons themselves, paid $960,000 to lobby for Joseph Schwartz's pardon. Schwartz was unhappy with their efforts and hired Josh Nass instead.
Source: WSJ / Wikipedia clemency list
$1M
Standard Entry Fee — The New Normal
Lobbyists have told the WSJ, NBC, and FT that $1 million is now the "standard rate" to get a pardon case in front of the right people in the White House.
Source: Wall Street Journal · Financial Times · NBC News
$1M
Keith Schiller & George Sorial — Developer Bribery
Former Trump bodyguard and Trump Org. exec received $1M in Q1 2025 to lobby for a developer convicted of bribing Sen. Robert Menendez with cash and gold bars.
Source: Wall Street Journal
$800K
Binance — Changpeng Zhao Pardon Lobby
Binance paid ~$800K in lobbying fees before Zhao's pardon was granted, plus agreed to potential "success fees of as much as $5M." The pardon wiped obligations beyond what Binance paid in fines.
Source: Wall Street Journal
up to $6M
Maximum Reported "Success Fee"
Pardon-seekers have offered lobbyists success fees as high as $6 million — paid only upon a successful pardon. Convictions wiping out hundreds of millions in restitution make even $6M a rational investment.
Source: Wall Street Journal · Raw Story

"We are seeing an industrial-scale pardon business. It's a separate process outside the criminal justice system for potentially getting your case to go away."
