Government's Rationale

Security Imperatives

Israeli officials argue that Hamas's continued control of Gaza poses an existential threat to Israeli civilians. The October 7, 2023 attack demonstrated Hamas's capability and intent to launch large-scale attacks against Israeli territory.

Self-Defense Framework

The government frames military operations as legitimate self-defense under international law, emphasizing the right to protect citizens from terrorist attacks and rocket fire from Gaza.

Hostage Recovery

Officials state that military pressure is necessary to secure the release of hostages held by Hamas and other groups in Gaza, with negotiations alone proving insufficient.

Territorial Control: Expanded military presence to prevent Hamas regrouping and weapon smuggling

4
Intelligence Operations: Gathering information on hostage locations and Hamas leadership
5/10

Intended Beneficiaries

Israeli Citizens

Enhanced security, end to rocket attacks, safe return of hostages, and restored deterrence against future attacks.

Palestinian Civilians

Israeli officials argue that removing Hamas will eventually benefit Palestinian civilians by ending authoritarian rule and enabling better governance and economic development.

Regional Stability

Government claims that eliminating Hamas will reduce regional tensions and prevent Iranian proxy influence from destabilizing the area.

6/10

Post-Conflict Governance Vision

Transition Period

Israeli officials envision a transitional period with international oversight and gradual transfer to alternative Palestinian leadership not affiliated with Hamas or other terrorist organizations.

Demilitarization Requirements

Any future governance must include guarantees of complete demilitarization, prevention of weapons smuggling, and robust security arrangements to prevent Hamas resurgence.

International Partnership

Israel seeks international partners, potentially including moderate Arab states, to help establish legitimate governance structures and economic development programs.

7/10

Economic Development Framework

Conditional Reconstruction

Israeli officials suggest that large-scale reconstruction aid should be contingent on establishing accountable governance and preventing resources from being diverted to military purposes.

Economic Incentives

The government proposes creating economic opportunities and improving living standards as alternatives to radicalization, potentially including work permits and trade relationships.

Infrastructure Security

Any rebuilt infrastructure must include security mechanisms to prevent dual-use for military purposes, particularly tunnels, ports, and communications systems.

8/10

Long-Term Regional Impact

Security Architecture

Establishment of new security arrangements preventing Iranian proxy influence and weapons smuggling throughout the region.

Diplomatic Opportunities

Potential for increased Arab-Israeli normalization as threats from radical groups diminish.

Deterrence Effect

Demonstration to other hostile groups that attacks on Israel will result in decisive military response and regime change.

Palestinian Self-Determination

Creation of conditions for legitimate Palestinian leadership to emerge free from Hamas intimidation and control.

9/10

International Cooperation Framework

Allied Support

Israeli officials emphasize coordination with key allies, particularly the United States, to ensure military operations align with broader strategic objectives and maintain international legitimacy.

Arab Partnership

Outreach to moderate Arab states to participate in post-conflict governance and reconstruction, leveraging Abraham Accords relationships and shared concerns about Iranian influence.

Humanitarian Coordination

Cooperation with international humanitarian organizations to ensure civilian needs are met while preventing aid diversion to military purposes.

10/10

Government's Strategic Vision

Core Premise

Israeli officials argue that decisive action against Hamas is necessary to restore deterrence, protect citizens, and create conditions for sustainable peace in the region.

Success Metrics

Government defines success as: elimination of Hamas military capabilities, return of all hostages, establishment of alternative governance, and long-term security for Israeli citizens.

Regional Transformation

The ultimate goal is described as transforming Gaza from a terrorist stronghold into a demilitarized territory with legitimate governance that contributes to regional stability rather than conflict.

Note: This presentation reflects the Israeli government's stated positions and rationale as expressed in official statements and policy documents. Different perspectives on these policies exist among international actors, humanitarian organizations, and other stakeholders.
