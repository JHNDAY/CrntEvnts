Economic Reality: Job Market at "Stall Speed" ▶
August Jobs Report Shows Dramatic Slowdown
Friday's devastating jobs report revealed only 22,000 new jobs in August versus an expected 75,000, with a loss of 13,000 jobs in July—the first loss since December 2020. Job growth over the last three months is only one-third the level of last year.

Manufacturing suffered a loss of 12,000 jobs in August and 78,000 year-to-date. Unemployment rose to 4.3%, the highest level since October 2021. For the first time in four years, there are fewer open jobs than job seekers.
"At this point, the job market is at stall speed."
— Claudia Sahm, Chief Economist for New Century Advisors

Immigration Policy Devastates Labor Force
According to Pew Research, 1.2 million immigrants dropped out of the labor force during the first seven months of this year. Immigrants account for 20% of the U.S. workforce, including 45% of employees in farming, fishing and forestry and 43% of home health aides.

Collectively, immigrants have accounted for 50% of U.S. job growth. Many of these jobs cannot readily be filled by non-immigrants, creating critical labor shortages.
Sources: Pew Research Center
Economic Growth Projections Dim
The annual inflation rate remained at 2.7% in July, compared to the 2.4% annual rate in September 2024 that Trump extensively criticized during his campaign. After experiencing 2.8% growth in 2024, Deloitte projects the U.S. economy will grow by only 1.4% this year.
Federal Reserve Crisis: Paul Krugman characterized Trump's efforts to assert dominance over the Federal Reserve as a "Wile E. Coyote moment," threatening economic stability.
Sources: Deloitte Economic Forecast

ICE Operations and Prison Expansions
An ICE raid on a Hyundai-LG automotive battery plant in Ellabell, GA, involving 500 law enforcement officers, resulted in 475 arrests of mostly undocumented South Koreans. The plant manufactured batteries for electric vehicles and was expected to produce 8,500 jobs in this rural area.
The administration plans to reopen three prison facilities in GA, KS and TX that were closed during the Biden administration due to excessive violence and mistreatment. A new ICE detention center with capacity for over 400 men has been authorized within the notorious Angola penitentiary in Louisiana.
