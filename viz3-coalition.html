✓ The Majority Held (6 Justices)
"IEEPA does not authorize the President to impose tariffs. Those words cannot bear such weight." — Chief Justice Roberts

Roberts, Gorsuch & Barrett invoked the Major Questions Doctrine — Congress must explicitly delegate powers of enormous consequence. Sotomayor, Kagan & Jackson agreed on the outcome via statutory interpretation alone, rejecting MQD but concurring in judgment. The decisive point: no president before Trump had ever read IEEPA to grant tariff power.
✗ The Dissent (3 Justices)
Thomas, Alito & Kavanaugh argued IEEPA's text, historical practice, and foreign affairs precedents all support presidential tariff authority. Kavanaugh warned the ruling could force refunds of $160B+ and destabilize existing trade agreements. He suggested other statutes may still authorize the same tariffs.
The Plot Twist: Gorsuch and Barrett — both Trump appointees, both confirmed by a Republican Senate — joined the majority and invoked the "Major Questions Doctrine," a conservative legal principle ironically used against the president who appointed them. Trump had previously praised this doctrine when it was used to strike down Biden's student loan forgiveness. "I think their decision was terrible… It's an embarrassment to their families, you want to know the truth, the two of them." — President Trump, Feb 20, 2026

Notably, Kavanaugh's dissent was arguably the most measured — he conceded the ruling might not "substantially constrain" tariff policy going forward, since other statutes exist. This may prove prophetic: see Visualization ④.
