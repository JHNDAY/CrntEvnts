Crisis vs. Problem: Menchik's Core Distinction

CRISIS

State Persecution

Government systematically persecutes widely disliked groups

  • Immigrants (ICE detentions)
  • Palestinians (visa suspensions)
  • Black Americans (systematic discrimination)

PROBLEM

Social Prejudice

Widespread prejudice without systematic state persecution

  • Antisemitism (social attitudes)
  • Normal feature of diverse societies
  • Rises during war/economic uncertainty

WEAPONIZATION

Authoritarian Tool

Antisemitism claims used to attack democratic institutions

  • Cancel medical research funding
  • Detain legal immigrants
  • Silence genocide discussions

Main Arguments

1. Authoritarian Context

US has descended into authoritarianism over past decade due to economic inequality, gerrymandering, and misinformation

2. Pluralistic Intolerance

Most people are intolerant toward some groups - this diversity actually prevents systematic persecution

3. Historical Perspective

Long-term data shows dramatic decreases in antisemitism over 60 years; current Jewish situation is relatively good

4. Comparative Analysis

Anti-Black violence 7× more common than anti-Jewish violence in Massachusetts; other minorities face worse persecution

Policy Recommendations

Proportionate Response

Remedy should match the scale of the problem, not manufactured crisis narratives

Reject Unreliable Data

Don't use ADL's flawed methodology that conflates criticism of Israel with antisemitism

Protect Minorities within Minorities

Include non-Zionist and anti-Zionist Jews in antisemitism discussions and protections

Avoid State Weaponization

Resist attempts to use antisemitism claims to silence democratic debate and criticism

Critique of ADL Data

Key Problems with ADL's 2023 Audit (205% increase claim):

  • 44% of incidents not clearly antisemitic according to Jewish Currents analysis
  • Conflates criticism of Israeli policy with antisemitism since 2023
  • No consistency in data collection across regions, time, or methods
  • Changed definition of antisemitic events in October 2023
  • Wikipedia editors declared ADL "unreliable" on antisemitism and Israel-Palestine

"No serious scholar of racism, bigotry, or antisemitism could get through peer review based on ADL's data. It's simply not credible."

Minorities within Minorities Problem

Policies designed to combat antisemitism can increase bullying and exclusion of Jews by other Jews, particularly non-Zionist, post-Zionist, and anti-Zionist Jews.

Academic Harassment

Jewish extremists from CAMERA and StandWithUs launched harassment campaigns against BU Jewish Studies faculty, sending thousands of emails during Rosh Hashanah

Hillel Exclusion

BU's Hillel displays Israeli flags and "We stand with Israel" signs, making non-Zionist Jews feel unwelcome despite needing kosher dining services

Community Ostracism

"In the American Jewish community today, it is easier to come out as gay than as non-Zionist or anti-Zionist"

Generational Shift: Survey data shows 30-40% of American Jews are now anti-Zionist or non-Zionist, with 30% differences between older and younger generations.
