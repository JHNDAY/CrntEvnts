Supreme Court & Presidential Power

Expanding Executive Authority

George's Key Question

Supreme Court has been expanding Trump's power significantly. If Democrats win in 2028, will the Court suddenly reconsider presidential power?
Geoffrey: Predicts Alito and Thomas will likely be replaced by younger equivalents by then.
"That thought has occurred to me also, George, more than once... It's just that there are Republican balls and strikes, and there are Democratic balls and strikes. And they get called different ways." - John

End-of-Term Concerns

Hal's Question: Will Trump do "really strange things" at end of his term? Speculates he could pardon everyone who buys one of his items - "he can do anything he wants, right?"
  • Reference to Blagojevich pardon - stayed Democrat, upset Trump
  • Uncertainty about how far Trump might push pardoning power
  • Constitutional checks may prove insufficient

Gerrymandering & Electoral Strategy

Republican Redistricting Advantages

Francesca: Republicans can come out ahead with "at least two more" districts due to gerrymandering.

Counter-Argument: Voter Disenchantment

Geoffrey/Hilary suggest that if Trump voters become disenchanted, Republicans may lose even re-gerrymandered seats. John agrees there's "a lot to that."

Houston Example

George: If there's a "semi-landslide" by Democrats, they could win some of the newly "contaminated" districts like those around Houston, Texas.

Cautious Optimism

While gerrymandering creates structural disadvantages, group acknowledges that genuine voter shifts could overcome these barriers.

Government Administration & Research Funding

Selective "Diversity" in Funding Distribution

Mark's Boston Globe Story

Major Boston hospitals complaining that government is diverting funds that normally go to them to colleges in Iowa and Nebraska.
"I thought the word diversity is not allowed in any context in this country anymore. We can't have diversity if it favors people of color or women, but I guess it's okay if it favors people in red states." - Mark
George: This is the policy of Jay Bhattacharya (mentored by Alan Garber at Harvard) who wants to distribute research funds throughout the country and not largely concentrated in places like Boston.
  • Reallocation of medical research funding away from major research hospitals
  • Geographic redistribution favoring red states
  • Ironic application of "diversity" concept to political redistribution
  • Questions about merit-based vs. political allocation of research resources

American History & National Character

Ken Burns' American Revolution Series

Context: Multiple participants discuss watching or planning to watch Ken Burns' American Revolution documentary. Mark notes MAGA people objecting to the series, calling it "woke."
"That tells... you know, that blew away a lot of the myths." - Geoffrey on Ken Burns' approach

Teaching History to Children

Balancing Truth and Pride

Geoffrey asks: "You have to give children some reason to have pride in their country, too, right?"
Sally's Response: What you teach third graders might be different than what you teach high school kids. "High school kids ought to know."

America's Dark History

  • Sally: "What about the way we've treated black people for the last 400 years? And what we did to all the Indians!"
  • George: Treatment of Native Americans is "even worse"
  • Mark: "Land and free labor" - the foundation of American wealth
"Our history is... there's so much propaganda, and they're not teaching a lot of the stuff in the schools... We've accomplished a lot, we had great resources, we're very powerful. But it seems like we've made a lot of messes everywhere we've gone." - Sally

John's Balanced View: Ken Burns & American Principles

Messy but Moving Forward

When Burns does documentaries, he tells a "truthful story of how messy our... the evolution of our country has been." But there's no doubt Burns has great respect and affection for the United States.
"If you watch his Civil War series... You'll see, in so many ways, the way in which Burns reveres American principles, particularly when it comes to Lincoln." - John

Historical Perspective on Current Crisis

George: "It's a little bit hard to feel that way now, John" - responding to Lincoln's idea of America as "best last hope of civilization."

John's Historical Context

It was hard to feel that way in the 1920s with the KKK everywhere, the American Nightmare. Various periods have been difficult (Know-Nothings, etc.), "but we have managed to move forward, and I think we'll move forward past the Trump years."

What Makes America Great

George's Key Point

"Many of the things that made us great are diversity. Immigrants who've made such big contributions... our higher educational system, et cetera, are all under attack by these Luddites."
Final Assessment: George concludes he's "more on [John's] side than Sally's" - thinks "we've been a pretty good country up until now." John acknowledges "it's a dark period."

Key Themes & Discussion Patterns

Institutional Concerns

Recurring Worries

  • Expansion of executive power through Supreme Court decisions
  • Partisan application of constitutional principles
  • Redistribution of resources for political rather than merit-based reasons
  • Manipulation of democratic systems through gerrymandering

Historical Consciousness

Pattern of Discussion

Group consistently places current events in broader historical context. References to Ken Burns, 1984, Lincoln, 1920s KKK, American Revolution demonstrate sophisticated historical awareness.

Tension: Truth vs. Patriotism

Central Question: How to balance teaching difficult historical truths with fostering civic pride, especially in young people. No clear resolution but thoughtful consideration of age-appropriate education.

Cautious Hope

Despite concerns about current situation, participants maintain belief that:
  • American institutions have weathered dark periods before
  • Voter disenchantment can overcome gerrymandering
  • The country will "move forward past the Trump years"
  • Core American principles remain worth defending
