The Intercept called the DHS-Medicaid data-sharing agreement "one of the most far-reaching domestic surveillance expansions in American history" — a merger of the social safety net with immigration enforcement that will deter millions of immigrants (including legal residents) from seeking healthcare. The publication documented legal challenges filed in Texas, Florida, and California and interviewed public health workers describing immediate chilling effects in their clinics.

Both outlets noted that the investigation is led by Harmeet Dhillon's Civil Rights Division — the same office critics accuse of redirecting civil rights enforcement away from historically protected groups.

Slate: IEEPA Ruling — Roberts Court Finds Its Limit
Slate's legal team provided the most detailed left-leaning analysis of the 6-3 IEEPA ruling, noting the unusual coalition (Roberts joined by both liberal justices and Gorsuch and Barrett) and the explicit invocation of the "major questions" doctrine to rein in executive power. Slate argued this represents a meaningful but incomplete victory — the same doctrine could be weaponized against progressive regulatory action in future cases, and the Court's tariff-specific ruling leaves many other executive overreaches intact.
