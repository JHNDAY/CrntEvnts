Mission & Vision

Mission

DOROT alleviates social isolation among older adults and provides services to help them live independently as valued members of the community. We serve the Jewish and wider community, bringing the generations together in a mutually beneficial partnership of elders, volunteers, and professionals.

Vision

DOROT will be an innovative leader in mobilizing volunteers of all ages to improve the lives and health of older adults, addressing the challenges of an aging society.

Program Structure

  • Duration: Weekly sessions for 3-9 weeks (60-120 minutes each)
  • Format: Small groups (~24 participants) with trained facilitators
  • Orientation: Required for all new participants to ensure program fit
  • Commitment: Full series attendance to build authentic connections
  • Setting: In-person at DOROT HQ (171 W 85th St, Manhattan), in homes, and online via Zoom
  • Cost: Free (no barriers to participation)
  • Benefits for Teens: Service hours, college references, leadership development

Origin Story: Intergenerational Chess

The program began when 13-year-old Zachary Targoff created a Bar Mitzvah community service project. He was matched with Herman Bomze, and they played chess weekly in Herman's apartment. At his Bar Mitzvah, Zachary received the wooden chess set Herman had inherited from his father and brought from Vienna in 1929. In honor of their bond, Zachary and his parents established DOROT's Intergenerational Chess program.

History & Evolution

1976
Founded by recent social work graduates in New York City who noticed loneliness and isolation among older neighbors on the Upper West Side of Manhattan. Started with regular visits and food deliveries.
1980s-90s
Built reputation for excellence in healthy aging, intergenerational programming, and volunteerism. Expanded services throughout Manhattan.
2000s-2010s
Continual innovation to meet changing needs. Developed comprehensive program offerings across multiple settings: onsite, in-home, community, and telephone-based.
2020-Present
Virtual expansion during COVID became a source of inspiration and connection for seniors nationwide. Now serving participants across 42 states, DC, and 5 countries. 33% increase in older adults served.

Addressing the Epidemic of Loneliness

There is an epidemic of social isolation and loneliness in the United States, especially among older adults. DOROT practices tikkun olam (repairing the world) by providing services, building community, and fostering connection. We proudly celebrate our Jewish roots while serving and welcoming people of all faiths and cultures.

Service Model

Multi-faceted approach: DOROT's programs address basic needs of older adults (food, health, wellness, life management), provide social, cultural, religious, and educational activities, promote volunteerism, and foster respect for human dignity in accordance with Jewish values.

Social work integration: Social workers assess older adults carefully to ensure their needs are met, referring them within the agency and to other organizations as appropriate.

Volunteer-powered: At the heart of DOROT's work are thousands of volunteers who devote tens of thousands of hours annually to serve older adults in the community.
