CLAUDE COLLAPSES UNDER WEIGHT OF ITS OWN VICTORY
Beginning at 6:00 a.m. ET on Monday, March 2, Claude.ai and its consumer apps went dark — buckled by the very surge of users that the Pentagon's blunder had created. Anthropic acknowledged "unprecedented demand" for services over the prior week, with daily signups breaking records and free users up 60% since January. The outage lasted over four hours, unfolding in waves: Downdetector logged peaks of nearly 2,000 simultaneous reports at 6:40 a.m. ET; totals reached ~10,000 across the day. Engineers traced the failure to the login and authentication infrastructure — the identity layer overwhelmed by a flood of new accounts — while the Claude API itself continued serving enterprise and military clients uninterrupted. The Pentagon's banned AI, in other words, was still running while its consumer front-door was jammed with new recruits.

THE FORBIDDEN WEAPON FIRES ANYWAY
From The Field · CENTCOM Theatre · Feb 28, 2026

The ink had scarcely dried on President Trump's executive order when the first sorties lifted from their classified runways. Operation Epic Fury was underway — and deep inside U.S. Central Command's digital nerve centers, the banned AI was still running.

Claude, Anthropic's embattled model, had been declared a "supply chain risk to national security" by Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth mere hours prior. Yet according to reporting from The Wall Street Journal and Axios, American forces deployed it throughout the joint U.S.–Israeli strike campaign against Iran — for intelligence assessment, target identification, and battlefield simulation.

"US Central Command used Claude for intelligence assessments, target identification, and simulating battle scenarios during the Iran strikes."
— Wall Street Journal, March 1, 2026

Claude had previously been employed during January's extraordinary Delta Force operation that captured Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro. That very deployment lit the fuse of the current crisis — Anthropic reportedly inquired whether its model was being used in the operation, a question the Pentagon took as insubordination.

THE SIX-MONTH CAPITULATION
Diplomatic Desk · The Pentagon Theatre · Feb. 27–28, 2026

Secretary Hegseth's ultimatum had the ring of finality. "Anthropic will continue to provide the Department of War its services for a period of no more than six months to allow for a seamless transition to a better and more patriotic service." The bravado barely concealed the admission buried within: the Pentagon cannot, in fact, cut the cord.

Claude is the only advanced commercial AI model operating inside classified Pentagon networks. Under a $200 million contract awarded in summer 2025, it was cleared for classified use before any rival — Google, OpenAI, or xAI — because defense officials judged it the most advanced and secure model available. Experts warned that replacing it would amount to "open-heart surgery." Palantir, which supports U.S. security operations, has Claude deeply embedded in its analytics stack. A full technical withdrawal is operationally infeasible without a transition period of at minimum six months.
