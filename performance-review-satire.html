— The Bit —

Straight A's. Allegedly.

Big news out of Washington tonight, folks. The President released his own performance review. He graded himself. (beat) Which is a little like letting the fox fill out the Yelp page for the henhouse — and the fox gives it one star "because parking was tight."

And here's the part I can't get over. He graded himself. By himself. With nobody watching. And he still came home with four F's. That takes a special kind of talent. Most people, when they're cheating off their own test, manage at least a C.

[ audience loses it ]

Let's go category by category, because I want to give this the full Oscar-night treatment. First up — Economy. An F. The good news is, it's the only number around here that's actually gone down.

Iran Deal — also an F. He says it was the greatest deal ever negotiated. The proof? A piece of paper with an F on it that he is holding up. On purpose. In front of cameras. I've seen ransom notes with better optics.

Cognition — F. Now, in fairness, he says he aced that one. He just can't quite recall when he took it, where he took it, or what the questions were. (beat) Which… honestly… kind of settles it.

And finally, Public Approval — F. His approval rating is now so low it qualifies for a tax cut. Scientists are searching for it. Last seen near the Mariana Trench.

[ band hits the rimshot ]

And then — at the bottom — in big gold letters, with little wreaths on either side like he just won the Kentucky Derby — one word. PERFECT.

Four F's. One gold trophy. That, my friends, is grading on a curve. And the curve… is a full circle. He failed so completely he lapped himself and came out the other side a winner. It's the participation trophy's evil twin.
