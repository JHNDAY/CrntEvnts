Proposed Topic: ICC Sanctions

One topic which I think might be of interest is the ramifications of Trump's sanctioning the ICC judges and prosecutors and Francesca Albanese, the UN Rapporteur on Palestine.

These sanctions mean:

  • These people are barred from entering the U.S.
  • They have no access to any financial institutions with a Swift Code (every bank in the world except Russia)
  • They cannot access bank accounts, use credit cards, call an Uber, or carry out any financial transactions
  • Any bank doing business with these people will also be sanctioned

Most of us think of sanctions as applying to bad countries or criminal oligarchs who don't rely on banking systems anyway, but to sanction ordinary citizens like these judges is unprecedented.

Constitutional Question: This is clearly depriving these people of their property without due process in violation of the Fifth Amendment, which applies to all people, not just Americans.

Emergency Powers & Presidential Authority

Trump issued Emergency Order No. 14203, based on the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) and the National Emergencies Act.

Sally's warning: "Trump has broad power over foreign affairs. By simply saying that a person has interfered with his managing foreign affairs, he can do pretty much anything he wants."

The concerning implication: He is only one step away from sanctioning American citizens using the same rationale.

Precedent
Historical Context

Unprecedented Use of Sanctions

Traditionally, sanctions target:

  • Hostile nations
  • Criminal oligarchs
  • Terrorists and international criminals

Sanctioning international judges and UN officials for doing their jobs represents a dangerous expansion of executive power and attacks on international institutions.
