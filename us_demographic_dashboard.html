Answer: Even with a below-replacement birth rate (1.6 vs. 2.1 needed), births still exceed deaths in absolute terms — adding ~500K people. Legal immigration adds ~1.3M more. The total reaches ~1.8M growth. The paradox dissolves.

Panel II · The Measurement Problem
Are You Comparing Apples to Apples?
Dimension Immigrants IN Citizens OUT
Who is counted All foreign-born arrivals: legal permanent residents, refugees, asylum seekers, temp workers, undocumented Only U.S. citizens who formally depart — a narrow slice
How measured Customs, visa records, DHS data — relatively trackable Estimated indirectly — the U.S. does not require exit reporting
Scale (2024–25) ~1.3 million net ~100,000 estimated
Verdict NOT apples to apples. The first inquiry compared a broad, well-measured category to a narrow, poorly-measured one. Net migration is still strongly positive.

The earlier Claude response about "net negative migration" likely referred to a specific subcategory or time window — not the overall 2024–25 picture, which shows net positive migration of ~1.3M.

Panel IV · Data Reliability Audit
Where Should You Trust the Numbers — and Where Should You Be Cautious?
Birth Rate & Fertility (1.6)
Reliable CDC vital statistics are among the most robust demographic data the U.S. collects.
Caution The 2.1 replacement-rate threshold assumes zero net immigration — it's a mathematical benchmark, not a policy target.
Caution Fertility rates vary significantly by region, education level, and immigrant vs. native-born status.
Immigration Inflow
Reliable Legal permanent residents and visa holders are well-documented via DHS records.
Unreliable Undocumented immigration is estimated, not counted — estimates vary widely by source and political framing.
Caution 2025 data is in flux due to rapidly changing enforcement policy — figures may be revised substantially.
Emigration of U.S. Citizens
Unreliable The U.S. has no mandatory exit tracking system. Emigration figures are estimates derived from foreign census data and surveys.
Unreliable "Americans leaving" figures frequently conflate temporary abroad residence with permanent emigration.
Caution This is the least reliable number in the entire inquiry — treat with significant skepticism.
