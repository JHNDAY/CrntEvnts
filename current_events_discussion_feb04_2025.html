‚öñÔ∏è Don Lemon Arrest: Press Freedom Under Assault

‚ñº
Sally analyzed the legal dimensions of Don Lemon's arrest, with Pam Bondi herself leading the charge on dubious charges

üì∞ The Incident

There was a protest at a church in Minnesota. Don Lemon and another journalist, Georgia Fort, were interviewing people at the protest. About 8 people were arrested. What made this particularly notable: Attorney General Pam Bondi herself led the charge‚Äî"very unusual, because Attorney Generals don't usually do that."

üö´ Three Courts Said No, Then Grand Jury

The Shopping Spree: Prosecutors in Minnesota went to 3 different courts trying to bring the indictment. All three threw it out. "One of the courts actually was a very right-wing judge, federal judge" who even took it to the appeals court for advice.

Grand Jury Workaround: Unable to get judicial approval, "they then gathered a bunch of laypeople for a grand jury, and the prosecutors have a lot of leeway, what they say, and what they present to the grand jury." We don't know what was presented, "but they got their indictment."

Timing: At this point, Don Lemon was in Los Angeles covering the Grammy Awards.

üìã The Charges

1. FACE Act (Freedom of Access to Clinic Entrances Act): This law applies to people who disrupt or prevent access to abortion clinics. "When it was being passed, there was a compromise, and they added a provision to say that you can't interfere or disrupt church services. And that was kind of an add-on."

2. Conspiracy Law (18 USC 241): "Pretty much the same thing" as FACE. Notably, "that law actually has death penalty as a possible punishment."

üìù The "29 Acts" of Alleged Violations

Alleged "Monstrous" Acts:

  • Posting on Instagram
  • Live streaming on his show
  • Stating he was "keeping his target secret" (the church)
  • Disrupting services
  • Gesturing, charging elderly and children
  • Blocking a minivan of children

The Problem: "The thing about the FACE statute is you have to physically do something. You can be noisy, but you have to actually physically do something." Sally noted: "If you've seen the videos of Don Lemon interviewing people, maybe he was standing too close, I have no idea, but it didn't look like doing anything to me. So, the whole thing is just sort of a fabrication."

üì∞ Freedom of the Press: The Critical Distinction

The Journalist Question: They made a big deal about whether Lemon is a journalist since he's independent, not employed by a major outlet.

Speech vs. Press: Sally explained the crucial difference. "The First Amendment protects people for speech, and freedom of the press." But why both?

"Freedom of the press is really the important one. The whole idea of freedom of speech is to be able to criticize your government. Nobody cares if you yell fire in a theater... But being able to criticize the government is absolutely critical for democracy."

International Context: "Where you see it, the abuse of... in countries that are supposedly democratic is in just about every country in Southeast Asia. They use anti-defamation, criminal defamation laws to either put people in prison or put them into mental institutions when they criticize the government."

Closer to Home: "We have a bunch of states, including Massachusetts, we have a criminal defamation statute. Now, nobody's... won't take Trump long to figure it out, how you get around freedom of the press by applying, you know, criminal defamation."

Sally's Conclusion: "I don't think it makes a difference whether he works for a newspaper, or he's independent. And I think they've kind of dropped that distinction."

üí¨ Group Discussion

Context from Francesca: "The priest, I think, is connected, or is an agent of ICE, and so that's one of the reasons they were there to begin with, the people that were protesting."

Historical Context from George: "In the 18th and well into the 19th century, the press is what mattered. That's the vehicle for which people got the news... Freedom of the press... mattered the most." Sally agreed: "It still does."

ICE Comparison: George noted that Lemon's enumerated "offenses" are "committed all over the country, every day, by ICE, in a far more egregious manner."

Sally's Prognosis: "There was a reason three courts refused to entertain this indictment. So, I don't think it's gonna go anywhere, to be honest. It'll be yesterday's news."

üéØ The Chilling Effect Achieved

George captured the essential point: "Even if it doesn't prevail in court, Sally, this is intimidating journalists, so they've already accomplished much of their goal." The case may fail legally, but the message is sent‚Äîcovering protests, especially those involving ICE or Trump administration actions, carries personal risk. Three courts rejected the case, yet a grand jury approved it. Pam Bondi herself led the charge. The criminal defamation laws Sally mentioned exist in many states, including Massachusetts, waiting to be weaponized. The legal analysis matters, but the intimidation is the point.
