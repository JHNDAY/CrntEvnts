FBI Raid on Fulton County Elections & Federal Voter Roll Demands ▶
FBI Seizes 2020 Election Records from Fulton County in Unprecedented Raid
On January 28, 2026, FBI agents executed a search warrant at the Fulton County Election Hub and Operation Center in Georgia, removing hundreds of boxes of ballots and records related to the 2020 presidential election. Director of National Intelligence Tulsi Gabbard was reported to be present at the raid, raising questions about the involvement of the intelligence community in an elections matter.
The Brennan Center for Justice documented the raid as part of a broader pattern, noting that the DOJ had sued Fulton County over its refusal to produce records related to the 2020 election. The Brennan Center described the effort as an unprecedented encroachment on states' constitutional power to run elections.
Newsweek reported that U.S. Senators described the FBI seizure of voting records from Fulton County as alarming to election officials nationwide, and noted that federal courts in California and Oregon had already rejected DOJ lawsuits seeking voter data.
Key Facts — Federal Voter Data Campaign
  • At least 46 states and D.C. received demands for complete voter registration lists
  • DOJ has sued 24 states and D.C. for refusing to provide unredacted voter rolls
  • At least 11 states have provided or agreed to provide full voter data including SSN and driver's license numbers
  • Federal courts in California, Oregon, and Georgia rejected DOJ's voter roll lawsuits
  • Administration seeking to abolish mail-in voting and nationalize election administration
46+
States received DOJ demands for voter registration data
The Texas Tribune reported that Texas became the latest state to comply with the administration's demands, turning over data on approximately 18.4 million registered voters — including dates of birth, driver's license numbers, and partial Social Security numbers.
Stateline reported that U.S. District Court judges in California and Oregon dismissed DOJ lawsuits seeking voter information, with the California judge calling the government's request "unprecedented and illegal."
Fulton County Commission Chairman Robb Pitts denounced the FBI raid, telling federal officials to "stay the hell out" of the county's election operations. The county filed legal challenges to block the seizure.
Democracy Docket reported that the DNC warned 10 states they may have violated federal election law by entering into agreements with DOJ requiring them to remove voters from rolls within 45 days — potentially violating the National Voter Registration Act's 90-day protection window.
Sources: Brennan Center for Justice, Texas Tribune, Stateline/Pew, Newsweek, Democracy Docket, Axios
Don Lemon Arrest, Journalist Intimidation & Press Freedom Under Siege ▶
DOJ Arrests Don Lemon, Independent Journalist Georgia Fort Under FACE Act
On January 30, 2026, former CNN anchor Don Lemon was arrested in Beverly Hills by federal agents and charged with conspiracy against religious freedom and violations of the FACE Act (Freedom of Access to Clinic Entrances) in connection with his coverage of an anti-ICE protest at Cities Church in St. Paul, Minnesota on January 18. Independent journalist Georgia Fort was also arrested at her home in Minneapolis as her three daughters watched. Attorney General Pam Bondi personally directed the arrests.
NBC News reported that the church protest was prompted by the discovery that one of Cities Church's pastors serves as an ICE official. Lemon and Fort were among dozens of people who entered the church; both maintained they were there as journalists documenting the event. Fort told NBC that armed federal agents surrounded her home at 6 a.m. while her children slept.
CBS News reported that a federal magistrate judge had previously rejected arrest warrants for Lemon, finding no probable cause. The DOJ then appealed to the Chief Judge of the U.S. District Court in Minnesota, who also declined. An appeal to the 8th Circuit Court of Appeals was similarly denied. Only after three courts rejected the case did prosecutors secure an indictment through a grand jury.
Three Courts Rejected Charges: A federal magistrate, a district court chief judge, and the 8th Circuit Court of Appeals all declined to authorize arrest warrants before prosecutors obtained a grand jury indictment.
CNN reported that Lemon vowed to fight the charges, stating outside the federal courthouse in Los Angeles: "I have spent my entire career covering the news — I will not stop now." His attorney, Abbe Lowell, called the arrest "this unprecedented attack on the First Amendment." The Committee to Protect Journalists condemned the arrest as something that "should alarm all Americans."
The Intercept described the prosecution as the Justice Department weaponizing a law intended to protect abortion clinic access in order to punish reporters covering anti-ICE activism. The only allegation against Fort was that she approached the pastor and tried to ask him questions — characterized by prosecutors as intimidation.
AI-Manipulated Mugshot of Nekima Levy-Armstrong Distributed by White House
Nekima Levy-Armstrong, a prominent Minneapolis civil rights leader also arrested in connection with the church protest, had her booking photograph manipulated before being distributed through White House social media channels. According to discussion participants and news reports, the altered image darkened her skin, enlarged her face, added artificial tears, and depicted her hair in disarray — dramatically distorting her actual appearance. The manipulated image was viewed millions of times before being identified as altered.
Discussion participants noted that the manipulated image was designed to evoke negative stereotypes and suggested it could strengthen Levy-Armstrong's legal case by demonstrating the malicious intent behind the government's actions.
The Cato Institute argued that the FACE Act itself may be unconstitutional as applied in this case, noting that if charges were brought in retaliation for protected First Amendment activity, the prosecution would violate constitutional rights.
Key Facts — Press Freedom Concerns
  • Lemon was arrested on January 30 in Beverly Hills while in town covering the Grammy Awards
  • Charges: Conspiracy against religious freedom and FACE Act violations
  • Protest stemmed from discovery that a pastor at Cities Church is a senior ICE official
  • Three courts rejected DOJ's initial attempts to obtain arrest warrants
  • Georgia Fort arrested at her home in front of her three children (ages 7–17)
  • Minnesota Attorney General Keith Ellison called the arrests "deeply troubling"
Sources: NBC News, CBS News, CNN, The Intercept, Cato Institute, NBC Los Angeles
Weaponizing the Administrative Subpoena ▶
Washington Post Investigation: Government Power Perverted Through Administrative Actions
A Washington Post investigative piece highlighted the Trump administration's use of administrative subpoenas — which are not court orders — to circumvent constitutional protections. The report detailed how the government is using bureaucratic instruments rather than judicial authorization to conduct ICE raids, seize election records, and intimidate journalists.
Discussion participants emphasized the constitutional significance: administrative actions lack judicial oversight and bypass the Fourth Amendment's protections against unreasonable search and seizure. As one participant noted, "You cannot break into somebody's house without a court order — it's against the Constitution, but it doesn't seem to be bothering the Trump administration."
Constitutional Concern: Administrative subpoenas are issued by government agencies without judicial review or court authorization, raising fundamental questions about due process when used for law enforcement actions such as home entries and records seizures.
Sources: The Washington Post (investigative report shared during session)
COVID-19 at Six Years: Deaths Declining, Vigilance Waning ▶
Six Years Since First U.S. Case: 1.2 Million Deaths and a Changing Landscape
The first U.S. COVID-19 case was identified in mid-January 2020 in the Seattle area, and the first death was recorded on February 28 of that year. Six years on, the pandemic has claimed over 1.2 million American lives — the highest reported national toll globally, exceeding the combined U.S. deaths from every war in American history (approximately 1.3 million, half from the Civil War).

The encouraging trend is unmistakable: annual deaths have fallen dramatically from their peaks, declining from 462,000 in 2021 to approximately 76,000, then 47,000, and just 16,000 in 2025 — below the pre-pandemic annual flu average of 35,000 deaths. This near-exponential decrease reflects the combined effects of vaccination, natural immunity from prior infections, and the generally milder characteristics of Omicron-family variants.
However, vaccination rates have plummeted. The national COVID vaccination rate for the current season stands at just 17% of adults, down from a peak of 80% at the end of 2021. Massachusetts, which historically led the nation in vaccination rates, currently trails the national average at just 12%.

Wastewater Monitoring Shows Encouraging Decline; Data Reliability Questioned
Wastewater surveillance — which Massachusetts pioneered in 2020, two years before federal monitoring began — shows a pronounced downward trend in viral load. National wastewater levels have dropped to approximately 3.59 on the CDC's logarithmic scale, categorized as "low-moderate." The Northeast shows slightly elevated levels compared to the South, consistent with seasonal indoor congregation patterns during winter months.
Massachusetts wastewater data, measured in viral cells per milliliter, shows current levels at roughly 500 — down from a peak of approximately 10,000 during the Omicron wave in early 2022, a 95% reduction.
Data Integrity Concern: Discussion participants raised questions about the reliability of federal health data under HHS leadership by Robert F. Kennedy Jr., whose vaccine-skeptic positions and appointment of vaccine advisory board members with financial interests in alternative medicine have eroded confidence in official reporting.
Sources: CDC Wastewater Surveillance, Our World in Data, World Health Organization
Texas U.S. Senate Democratic Primary: Crockett vs. Talarico ▶
Two Rising Stars Compete for Democratic Nomination in March 3 Primary
The Texas Democratic primary for U.S. Senate features two candidates widely regarded as rising stars: U.S. Representative Jasmine Crockett of Dallas and state Representative James Talarico of Austin. The winner will face the Republican nominee — likely incumbent Senator John Cornyn or Attorney General Ken Paxton — in November, seeking to flip a seat no Democrat has won statewide since 1994.
The Texas Tribune profiled the candidates' contrasting approaches: Crockett, a civil rights attorney and second-term congresswoman, brings combative progressive energy and high national name recognition as the national co-chair of the Harris-Walz 2024 campaign. Talarico, a former middle school teacher and Presbyterian seminarian, flipped a Trump district to win his Texas House seat and campaigns on cross-partisan appeal to independents and disaffected Republicans.

Texas Tribune/TPOR polling (Jan. 14–21) shows the race in a statistical dead heat: Crockett at 38%, Talarico at 37%, with 21% undecided. However, polls have varied widely — an Emerson College poll (Jan. 10–12) gave Talarico a 9-point lead, while a Texas Southern University poll (December) had Crockett ahead by 8 points.

Texas Tribune reported that both candidates significantly outraise their Republican counterparts, with Talarico notably refusing corporate PAC money and emphasizing small-dollar grassroots fundraising. Both Democrats have raised more individually than any single Republican candidate's campaign account.

Texas State Senate District 9: Democrats Flip Deep-Red Seat in Stunning Upset ▶
Union Machinist Taylor Rehmet Defeats Trump-Endorsed Republican by 14 Points
On January 31, 2026, Democrat Taylor Rehmet defeated Republican Leigh Wambsganss 57%–43% in the special election runoff for Texas State Senate District 9, flipping a Fort Worth–area seat that Republicans had held since 1991. The result represented a staggering 31-point swing from the 2024 presidential results, in which Trump carried the district by 17 points.
31-Point Swing
From Trump +17 in 2024 to Rehmet (D) +14 in January 2026
The Texas Tribune reported that Rehmet, a 33-year-old union chapter president, Air Force veteran, and first-time candidate, won despite being massively outspent. Wambsganss, the chief communications officer of Patriot Mobile, raised $2.6 million — fueled by far-right oil billionaires Tim Dunn and Farris Wilks — compared to Rehmet's roughly $570,000.
CNN called the result a "big deal," noting that Tarrant County is considered a major national bellwether and that Rehmet actually performed better on Election Day (58–42) than in early voting (56–44) — despite Trump posting three separate get-out-the-vote messages for Wambsganss on social media in the 48 hours before the election.

Massachusetts Senate Primary: Markey vs. Moulton — A Generational Contest ▶
Moulton Challenges Incumbent Markey on Age; Pressley Looms as Wild Card
Representative Seth Moulton (age 46) launched his challenge to incumbent Senator Ed Markey (age 79) on October 16, 2025, framing the race as a generational transition. Markey would be 80 before the start of another six-year term if reelected. The September 1, 2026 primary will determine whether Massachusetts Democrats choose continuity with the progressive incumbent or a shift toward Moulton's more centrist approach.
UMass Amherst/WCVB polling shows Markey leading with 44% to Moulton's 25%, a 19-point advantage. Markey's favorability stands at a commanding +64, compared to Moulton's +29, with 32% of voters saying they haven't heard enough to form an opinion about Moulton.
Data for Progress (Oct. 23–26) found a similar 19-point Markey lead, and noted that even after respondents heard positive messaging about both candidates, Markey maintained a 13-point advantage. However, a UNH Survey Center poll found a smaller gap, and a Suffolk/Boston Globe survey showed the race tightening.
NBC Boston reported the most intriguing finding: in a hypothetical three-way race including Representative Ayanna Pressley, polling showed Pressley at 35%, Markey at 34%, and Moulton at 16% — suggesting Pressley could be competitive if she enters the race.
Key Facts — MA Senate Primary
  • Primary Date: September 1, 2026
  • Markey: Two-term incumbent, age 79, progressive, defeated Joe Kennedy III in 2020 primary
  • Moulton: 3rd-term congressman (6th District), age 46, Marine veteran, centrist, ran briefly for president in 2020
  • Central issue: Age and generational change — Markey leads among liberals, Moulton among moderates
  • Wild card: Rep. Ayanna Pressley polling competitively with Markey if she enters
Sources: UMass Amherst/WCVB Poll, Data for Progress, UNH Survey Center, NBC Boston / Suffolk-Globe Poll, Wikipedia, Ballotpedia
