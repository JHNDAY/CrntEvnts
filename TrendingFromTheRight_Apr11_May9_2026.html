03 Supreme Court & Other Legal Issues 40 mentions ▼
SCOTUS IEEPA Ruling — Tariffs Struck Down
The Supreme Court ruled 6-3 (Feb. 20) that IEEPA does not authorize the president to set tariffs, invalidating Trump's signature trade policy and requiring an estimated $166 billion in refunds to importers. Chief Justice Roberts's majority opinion invoked the "major questions" doctrine. Trump called the ruling a "disgrace" and moved to impose alternative tariffs under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974.
WSJ Trade National Review The Dispatch
Court of International Trade Blocks Section 122 Tariffs
After SCOTUS invalidated IEEPA tariffs, the administration imposed global 10% tariffs under Section 122. On May 7, the CIT issued a 2-1 ruling that these tariffs were also unjustified, dealing another blow to the White House trade agenda. The administration is now pivoting to Section 301 investigations to rebuild a durable tariff regime.
WSJ Trade Daily Wire NY Post
Federal Judges Blocking ICE — "Judicial Resistance"
Multiple federal judges — including three in New York — ordered the release of ICE-held detainees and attempted to block deportations. A California federal judge tried to shut down ICE enforcement operations on profiling grounds. A split three-judge panel rejected Trump's use of the Alien Enemies Act for peacetime deportations. Right-leaning commentators denounce this as an unconstitutional usurpation of executive power.
Breitbart Daily Wire NY Post
Courts vs. HHS Restructuring
A federal court issued an injunction barring HHS from further planning on its restructuring into the Administration for a Healthy America (AHA). Courts also blocked the administration's attempt to alter the childhood vaccine schedule. RFK Jr. acknowledged in congressional testimony that he has been "advised by attorneys not to talk" about the reorganization due to ongoing litigation.
