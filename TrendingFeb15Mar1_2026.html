SCOTUS Strikes IEEPA Tariffs 6-3 (Feb 20)
In a landmark 6-3 ruling, the Supreme Court held that Trump's use of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act to impose broad global tariffs exceeded executive authority under the non-delegation doctrine. The decision was written by Chief Justice Roberts; Justices Thomas and Alito dissented. Economists estimated a $134 billion refund liability for the federal government.
NYT Politics WaPo Courts WSJ Politics
Trump Attacks Gorsuch & Barrett by Name
Within hours of the tariff ruling, Trump posted Truth Social attacks on Justices Gorsuch and Barrett calling the decision "an embarrassment to their families" and demanding the ruling be revisited. Legal scholars across the political spectrum condemned the attacks as an unprecedented assault on judicial independence.
NYT Politics MSNBC H.C. Richardson
Section 122 Workaround & $134B Refund Battle
After the IEEPA ruling, Trump immediately announced a replacement tariff structure under Section 122 of the Trade Act, starting at 10% then rising to 15%. Legal challenges were filed within 24 hours.
