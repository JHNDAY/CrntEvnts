03 Supreme Court & Other Legal Issues 40 mentions ▼
SCOTUS 6–3: IEEPA Tariffs Unconstitutional (Feb 20)
In Learning Resources v. Trump, Chief Justice Roberts — joined by Gorsuch, Barrett, Kagan, Sotomayor, and Jackson — ruled that the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) does not authorize the president to impose tariffs. The 6–3 decision (Thomas, Kavanaugh, Alito dissented) strikes down $160B+ in collected tariffs and the "Liberation Day" duties. CNN reports Trump learned the news from a note while meeting with governors, said "that's a disgrace," and left the room. He later called the ruling "deeply disappointing" and said he was "ashamed" of certain justices he appointed.
CNN NPR NYT
Refund Battle: CBP Defies Court Order
Judge Eaton of the Court of International Trade ordered CBP to begin refunding illegally collected tariffs on Mar 4. Two days later, CBP filed a declaration saying it "is not able to comply" — calling the volume of refunds "unprecedented" and its systems "not well-suited." More than 2,000 lawsuits are pending. Trump says the issue "could be in court for up to five years." Democrats propose legislation requiring $175B in refunds with interest; Republicans block it. SCOTUSblog notes the refund question is likely to be a "mess."
Politico NYT NPR
Section 122 Replacement: 150-Day Tariff Clock
Within hours of the IEEPA ruling, Trump invoked Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 to reimpose a 10% global tariff for 150 days (expiring ~July 24, 2026). The Atlantic notes this authority is narrow, temporary, and already being challenged by a coalition of states arguing Trump has "not met the statutory prerequisites." Additional Section 232 and Section 301 investigations cover 15+ countries. A constitutional tariff fight extending into midterm season looks virtually certain.
The Atlantic NYT Politico
Election Law: SAVE Act, Mail-in Voting, VRA at SCOTUS
SCOTUS hears oral arguments in a Mississippi case on whether mail-in ballots received after Election Day may be counted; conservative justices expressed skepticism. A Voting Rights Act Section 2 case could restrict gerrymandering remedies before November. CNN analysis: the SAVE America Act (requiring in-person citizenship proof to register) combined with potential redistricting maps and ICE polling-place threats form a multi-front effort to shape the 2026 outcome. Multiple secretaries of state are preparing legal responses.
CNN NYT Politico
