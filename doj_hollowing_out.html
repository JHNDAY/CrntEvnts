A Physicist's Frame: Institutional Entropy

Complex systems degrade when they lose the distributed expertise and independence that maintain coherent function. What follows is an attempt to measure that degradation at the DOJ — not to assign blame, but to understand what has changed, at what rate, and with what observable consequences. The numbers matter. So do the open questions.

I
What the Evidence Shows
Documented, verifiable, sourced from multiple outlets across the spectrum
Staffing & Departures
A Wave of Resignations Begins in February 2025
Seven DOJ prosecutors resigned in February 2025 after being ordered to drop federal corruption charges against NYC Mayor Eric Adams. Acting Deputy AG Emil Bove directed the Southern District of New York to dismiss the case, citing Adams' cooperation with Trump's immigration agenda. Career prosecutors called the justification "pretextual."
~12 resignations
from SDNY and Public Integrity Section in this single episode alone.
Prosecutorial Priorities
23,000+ Criminal Cases Dropped; Focus Shifts to Immigration
ProPublica's analysis of two decades of DOJ data found the number of criminal case declinations under AG Bondi was unprecedented. Cases dropped included mortgage fraud, health care fraud, and nursing home investigations — not just immigration enforcement gaps.
~11,000
cases declined in February 2025 alone — the highest monthly total since at least 2004.
Court Findings
Federal Courts Find "Vindictive Prosecution" in Abrego Garcia Case
A federal judge dismissed the criminal case against Kilmar Abrego Garcia — a man mistakenly deported — ruling the charges amounted to vindictive prosecution. The court found evidence that DOJ leadership used the indictment as retaliation for a court order requiring his return. The judge wrote: "The evidence before this Court sadly reflects an abuse of prosecuting power."
Independence & White House Coordination
DOJ Legal Positions Reported to be Directed by White House
Multiple former DOJ officials told NPR that the White House was directing the department's positions in court and shaping language in legal filings. One senior official said both institutions "would be better off if there was more pushback" from Justice.
Staffing Decline & Expertise
DOJ Civil Division Offering $25,000 Signing Bonuses to Recruit Lawyers
Bloomberg Law reported the Civil Division — responsible for defending the government in court — began offering $25,000 signing bonuses and retention incentive allowances. The division reportedly struggles to attract qualified attorneys for politically directed cases.
Grand Jury Resistance
Grand Juries Refusing DOJ Charges in Politicized Cases
A growing number of federal grand juries have declined to indict on charges brought by politically directed DOJ prosecutors. In some cases, federal lawyers reportedly pursued extreme measures to force charges through over grand jury resistance.
Oversight Removal
17 Inspectors General Fired in Single Day
On January 24, 2025 — four days into the new administration — Trump fired at least 17 inspectors general at federal departments and agencies simultaneously. Eight of those fired filed a lawsuit challenging the removals as unlawful.
Leadership Turnover
AG Pam Bondi Fired April 2026; DOJ Under Third Leadership in 15 Months
Trump announced Bondi's departure in early April 2026, offering no specific reason. Her firing came weeks after a contentious congressional hearing. DOJ has now had multiple attorneys general and acting deputies within the first 15 months of the second term.

II
What Remains Contested or Uncertain
Where reasonable people with the same facts draw different conclusions
The Administration's Argument
  • The Biden DOJ was itself weaponized — targeting Trump and conservatives through politically motivated prosecutions.
  • AG Bondi and Trump argue they are ending weaponization, not beginning it.
  • Case declinations represent legitimate prosecutorial discretion, not obstruction.
  • The Adams case was a politically motivated prosecution by a Biden-era prosecutor.
  • Pardoning Jan. 6 defendants corrects an overcriminalized response to a protest.
  • DOJ independence is a norm, not a legal mandate — presidents have executive authority over DOJ.
The Critics' Argument
  • The scale and pattern of changes — not any single act — constitutes systemic erosion of independence.
  • Career prosecutors, not political appointees, resigned rather than follow orders: a structural signal.
  • Court findings (Adams, Abrego Garcia) provide objective, non-partisan validation of politicization claims.
  • Dropping 23,000 cases — including health and financial fraud — goes far beyond an immigration pivot.
  • Removing IGs eliminates the oversight infrastructure that makes independence verifiable.
  • Future administrations can prosecute current DOJ officials for documented misconduct.

Historical Analogues Worth Considering

Watergate / Nixon (1973)
Saturday Night Massacre: Nixon ordered the firing of Special Prosecutor Cox; AG and Deputy AG resigned rather than comply. Led to impeachment proceedings.
McCarthy Era (1950s)
Executive pressure on DOJ and FBI to pursue political enemies. Long-term damage to institutional credibility; eventual correction took years.
Reconstruction (1870s)
DOJ founded in 1870 specifically to enforce Reconstruction. Political rollback by subsequent administrations gutted enforcement; effects lasted decades.
Bush / Gonzales (2007)
US Attorney firings for political reasons prompted congressional investigation. Several DOJ officials resigned; AG Gonzales testified before Congress.
III
Questions Worth Sitting With
The physicist's third tier — designed to slow reaction and invite analytical pause

If DOJ independence is a norm rather than a law, what actually enforces it — and what happens when enforcement mechanisms are removed?

Systems & Structures

How would you distinguish "ending weaponization" from "redirecting weaponization"? What evidence would you need to see to tell the difference?

Epistemology

When career prosecutors resign rather than follow orders, what weight should we give that signal — compared to the word of political appointees?

Institutional Expertise

The 23,000 dropped cases include health fraud and financial crimes, not only immigration. Does the pattern matter more than any single case?

Pattern vs. Event

What mechanisms remain to hold the current DOJ accountable? Which are working? Which have been neutralized?

Accountability

If future litigation against the current DOJ succeeds after the administration ends, what would that tell us about whether norms are self-correcting?

Long Arc

Which historical parallel feels most apt to you — and what does your choice reveal about your assumptions?

Historical Reasoning

At what point, if any, does the loss of legal expertise become irreversible — the way a complex system can reach a tipping point past which entropy is self-sustaining?

Physicist's Question

Steelman Exercise — For the Facilitator

Before closing the session, ask the group to try this: Make the strongest possible case for the position you find hardest to hold. Not a caricature of it — the best version a thoughtful person might argue in good faith. If someone can't do this, they may be reacting rather than reasoning. The goal is not to change minds, but to widen the aperture through which everyone sees the evidence.
