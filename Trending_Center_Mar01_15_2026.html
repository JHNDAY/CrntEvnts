Section 122 & Section 301 — New Legal Battles
After the IEEPA defeat, Bloomberg tracked Trump's pivot to alternative legal authorities. Section 122 (balance-of-payments authority) allows a 15% global tariff for only 150 days; Section 301 investigations (launched against 15+ countries on March 11-12) take months and require elaborate procedural steps. CBP told a court it could not comply with the ordered IEEPA refund process; it proposed a new ACE-based system expected to be ready by mid-April. Bloomberg estimated the total IEEPA tariff refund exposure at $100–175 billion — a potential massive fiscal event if courts enforce it.

The de minimis case had been held pending the IEEPA ruling; now it proceeds as a separate track. Bloomberg noted the financial stakes for online retail and small importers are significant, with the case expected to proceed to a full hearing.
