2025: The Year Constitutional Democracy Fought Back

A Chronicle of Authoritarian Overreach and Popular Resistance
Author: Heather Cox Richardson
Published: December 30, 2025
Context: Year-end analysis of Trump's first year in second term
⚠️ CRITICAL CONTEXT: This piece is written as if from December 30, 2025, describing the year's events in past tense. Like the Thiessen piece, this is speculative fiction presented as historical analysis—a predictive scenario of what the author fears could happen, not a report of actual events.

Central Thesis

Richardson's Core Argument: "The hallmark of the first year of President Donald J. Trump's second term has been the attempt of the president and his cronies to dismantle the constitutional system set up by the framers of that document when they established the United States of America. It's not simply that they have broken the laws. They have acted as if the laws, and the Constitution that underpins them, don't exist."

Narrative Arc: From Triumph to Resistance

Phase 1: Post-Election Power Grab (Nov-Dec 2024)

Musk declares "New World Order"; Trump claims "unprecedented mandate" despite <50% vote; DOGE established to cut $2 trillion from budget

Phase 2: Constitutional Violations Accelerate (Jan-Apr 2025)

Unilateral tariffs, emergency declarations, impoundment of funds, mass deportations, USAID dismantling

Phase 3: Popular Resistance Emerges (Apr-Jun 2025)

"Hands Off" rallies (Apr 5), "No Kings" protests (June 14), Tesla Takedowns weaken Musk

Phase 4: Momentum Shifts (Jul-Sep 2025)

"One Big Beautiful Bill" passes but support drops; Trump health crisis; military deployments backfire

Phase 5: Law and Democracy Reassert (Oct-Dec 2025)

7+ million march; Democrats sweep elections; Epstein files force bipartisan break; courts rule against administration

Timeline of Key Events

November 2024
Power Grab

Post-Election: Elon Musk posts "Novus Ordo Seclorum" (New World Order). Trump claims "unprecedented mandate" despite winning less than 50% of vote. Musk appointed to head "Department of Government Efficiency" with goal to cut $2 trillion.

Early 2025
Constitutional Violation

DOGE Invasion: Musk and operatives gain access to government computer systems, eliminate jobs with "strokes of a keyboard." USAID "fed into the wood chipper"—dismantling causes "deaths of hundreds of thousands of people." Nearly 10% of federal workforce eliminated. Operatives placed in all agencies, gaining access to privileged information on citizens and immigrants.

February 2024-Ongoing
Legal Resistance

Legal Groundwork: Democratic attorneys general begin writing lawsuits challenging anticipated executive orders. Judges begin ruling against administration policies.

March 2025
Defying Courts

CECOT Deportations: Administration renders 230+ immigrants (mostly Venezuelans, nearly half with legal status) to notorious CECOT terrorist prison in El Salvador—after federal judge orders them not to. Kilmar Ábrego García case becomes focal point: both federal judge and Supreme Court order his return, but administration refuses. This defiance of judicial orders crystallizes public anger.

April 2025
Emergency Declarations

Tariff War Launched: Trump declares nine "emergencies" to act without congressional oversight, following Carl Schmitt's authoritarian theory that power belongs to leaders who exploit emergencies. Speaker Johnson manipulates House rules to prevent challenge votes. Trump launches tariff war, usurping Congress's constitutional authority over trade.

April 5, 2025
Mass Protests Begin

"Hands Off" Rallies: Coalition of civil rights organizations, women's groups, labor unions stage nationwide rallies against dismantling of government services. "Tesla Takedowns" campaign targets Musk.

June 14, 2025
First "No Kings" Day

Trump's 79th Birthday: On same day as 250th anniversary of U.S. Army, Trump plans military parade in Washington. Protesters organize counter-events under banner "No Kings"—referencing America's founding rejection of monarchy. Trump's popularity dropping.

June 2025
Military Deployment

Los Angeles: Trump sends federalized National Guard and Marines to LA, against Governor Newsom's wishes, allegedly to protect federal officials from deportation protest violence.

July 2025
One Big Beautiful Bill

Republican Policy Triumph: Omnibus law extends 2017 tax cuts for wealthy/corporations while cutting Medicaid, SNAP, ACA subsidies. Passed via budget reconciliation (no Democratic votes). Three Republicans oppose; Vance casts deciding vote. Meanwhile, Trump personally profits from tariff discussions, golf course permissions, cryptocurrency investments, donations from those with government business. Congress attempts oversight but officials refuse to cooperate, treating members with contempt.

August 26, 2025
Explicit Authoritarianism

Trump's Declaration: "I have the right to do anything I want to do. I'm the president of the United States. If I think our country's in danger—and it is in danger in these cities—I can do it." Echoes Vance's 2024 statement on Carl Schmitt: "There's no law, there's just power."

September 2025
Military Deployment

Portland: Trump deploys National Guard troops to Portland, Oregon.

Labor Day Weekend 2025
Health Crisis

Trump Disappears: President disappears for several days. His deteriorating physical and mental health becomes major story.

October 2025
Military Deployment

Chicago: National Guard deployed to Chicago. Curtis Yarvin (influential right-wing theorist) declares the "second Trump revolution...is failing" because it hasn't gone far enough or fast enough.

October 18, 2025
Massive Resistance

Second "No Kings" Day: More than 7 million people take to streets nationwide demonstrating opposition to Trump administration. Largest protests of the year.

October 20, 2025
Symbolic Destruction

White House Demolition: With disapproval at near-historic levels, Trump begins bulldozing East Wing of White House (the "People's House").

November 2025
Electoral Repudiation

Democratic Sweep: Voters strongly back Democrats. Democratic governors win Virginia and New Jersey by double digits; nearly every district moves away from Republicans. NYC and Miami elect Democratic mayors (Miami's first in nearly 30 years). Republican supermajorities broken in Iowa and Mississippi state senates. Over entire 2025, Democrats flip 21% of Republican-held seats on ballots.

November 19, 2025
Bipartisan Break

Epstein Files Transparency Act: After AG Pam Bondi refuses to release Epstein files, bipartisan team launches discharge petition overriding Speaker Johnson. Congress passes act with overwhelming vote—"significant break of Republicans from Trump"—requiring DOJ to release files by December 19.

December 19, 2025
Defying Law

Deadline Missed: Administration fails to meet legal deadline for Epstein files release. However, material released shows Trump "more deeply involved with Epstein and his activities than he has admitted." Wall Street Journal reveals Mar-a-Lago spa sent young women to Epstein's house where he exposed himself and pressured them for sex; Ghislaine Maxwell recruited through the spa.

December 22, 2025
Courts Assert Authority

CECOT Ruling: Federal judge orders Trump administration to file plan to return men sent to CECOT or hold hearings, insisting on due process rights.

December 23, 2025
Supreme Court Rejection

National Guard Deployment: Supreme Court issues preliminary rejection of Trump's justification for deploying National Guard troops in Illinois.

Constitutional Violations Catalog

Power of the Purse (Congress → Executive)

Constitution, judicial precedent, and 1974 Impoundment Control Act make clear Congress controls spending. DOGE eliminated programs and spending with "strokes of a keyboard," usurping congressional authority. Democrats objected; Republicans backed it as eliminating "waste, fraud, and abuse."

Tariff Authority (Congress → Executive)

Constitution reserves trade/tariff authority to Congress alone. Trump launched tariff war in April under emergency declarations, taking this power unilaterally.

Emergency Abuse (Constitutional Process → Schmittian Exception)

Despite controlling both chambers of Congress, Trump declared nine "emergencies" to bypass congressional oversight. Based on Carl Schmitt's theory that leaders gain power through creating exceptions to constitutional order. Trump: "I have the right to do anything I want to do."

Judicial Defiance (Executive ignoring Courts)

CECOT deportations proceeded after federal judge ordered administration not to. Administration refused both federal judge and Supreme Court orders to return Kilmar Ábrego García. Administration failed to meet legally-mandated December 19 deadline for Epstein files.

Congressional Oversight Obstruction

Administration officials "treated members with contempt, refusing to appear or declining to answer questions, talking over them, or insulting them."

Privacy Violations

DOGE operatives gained "access to privileged information about Americans, including citizens, legal residents, and undocumented immigrants." Breaching of privacy laws by DOGE staffers.

Posse Comitatus / Federalism Violations

Federalized National Guard and Marines sent to LA (June), Portland (September), Chicago (October), and Illinois—against wishes of state governors. "Trump took federal control of D.C. police."

The "One Big Beautiful Bill" (July 2025)

Content: Omnibus legislation implementing long-promised Republican ideology:

  • Extended 2017 tax cuts benefiting primarily wealthy and corporations
  • Cut Medicaid
  • Cut SNAP (food stamps)
  • Cut ACA marketplace insurance subsidies

Process: Passed under budget reconciliation (cannot be filibustered); no Democratic votes. Three Republicans voted against; Vance cast deciding vote.

Context: While bill passed, Trump personally profited from position—leveraged tariff discussions for golf course permissions, cryptocurrency investments, donations from those with government business.

Deportation Reality vs. Rhetoric

Trump's Promise: Deport "the worst of the worst" criminal undocumented immigrants raping and murdering U.S. citizens.

Reality: Masked ICE and Border Patrol agents arrested:

  • Foreign students who spoke out against U.S. Israel/Palestine policy
  • Any undocumented immigrants they could find
  • People well-integrated into communities
  • Few charged or convicted of crimes

Result: Sweeping raids illustrated Trump's promise was "a lie."

Richardson's Conclusion

"As we reach the end of 2025, it appears the law is catching up to an administration that began the year by acting as if the law and the Constitution didn't exist."

"More than that, though, over the course of 2025, the administration's refusal to recognize the tenets of American democracy has roused the American people to defend that democracy."

"It appears that as we approach the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence, when British colonists on the North American continent took the radical step of rejecting the idea not just of King George III but of all kings, and launched the experiment of government based on the rule of law created by the people themselves, the American people are reclaiming that history."

Historical Framing: The "No Kings" Theme

Richardson frames the year's resistance movement around the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence (July 4, 2026) and foundational American rejection of monarchy.

Key Dates:

  • June 14, 2025: 250th anniversary of U.S. Army—Trump plans military parade, protesters declare "No Kings"
  • October 18, 2025: 7+ million march under "No Kings" banner
  • July 4, 2026: Upcoming 250th anniversary of Declaration of Independence

Symbolism: American founding was rejection "not just of King George III but of all kings"—government based on rule of law created by the people, not rule by strongman claiming unlimited power.

Contemporary Application: Trump's August 26 statement ("I have the right to do anything I want to do") and administration's defiance of courts, Congress, and Constitution represents return to monarchical principles that America explicitly rejected in founding.
