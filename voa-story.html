Voice of America: Down but Not Out

Buttressed by courts and support from some Republican lawmakers, federally funded newsrooms that President Trump has tried to eliminate have yet to be rendered obsolete
By Minho Kim | October 26, 2025 | The New York Times
For nearly two years, Voice of America provided intensive Persian-language coverage of Israel's war with Hamas, regularly reaching viewers inside Iran with reporting Tehran typically suppresses. But in June 2025, Iranians tuning in got something different: a two-hour live broadcast of a U.S. military parade celebrating the Army's 250th anniversary. This marked a symbolic turning point for the news agency founded in 1942 to fight Nazi propaganda‚Äînow targeted for destruction by President Trump.

The Controversy

President Trump has called V.O.A. "a total left-wing disaster" and signed an executive order in March effectively dismantling its parent organization.

Trump Administration's Position

White House spokeswoman Abigail Jackson stated that V.O.A. spreads "left-wing propaganda" and that Americans are "better off not being forced to waste money on that garbage."

The battle represents a broader debate: Should government-funded newsrooms promote American values through independent journalism, or should they be tools to promote administration policy?

National Security Implications

Some national security experts have expressed alarm that Russian and Chinese state broadcasters would attract the millions of listeners V.O.A. once had.

  • China and Russia each spend billions annually on propaganda, compared to $850 million for U.S.-funded newsrooms in 2024
  • Radio jamming: China added 80+ new frequencies to expand coverage and jam Radio Free Asia's waves
  • Enemy celebration: Chinese state media called V.O.A. "a dirty rag"; Russia Today editor celebrated closure as "a holiday"
  • Coverage vacuum: 360 million weekly V.O.A. listeners + 150 million from sister networks now without trusted news source
