November 5, 2025 Off-Year Elections

Overview

The November 5, 2025 off-year elections marked a significant turning point for Democrats, delivering decisive victories across gubernatorial, mayoral, judicial, and legislative races nationwide. These results represented the first major electoral test of the Trump administration's second term and showed widespread voter dissatisfaction with the direction of the country.

Virginia Abigail Spanberger HISTORIC

Result: Democrat Abigail Spanberger defeated Republican Winsome Earle-Sears with approximately 57.5% of the vote, becoming Virginia's first woman governor.
Why It's Important: The victory came in a state Trump lost by only 5.7 points just a year earlier, showing Democrats significantly outperforming their 2024 margins. Spanberger ran on a pledge to protect Virginia's economy from Trump's second administration, which has implemented tariffs and curtailed health care systems. Her campaign focused relentlessly on lowering costs, keeping communities safe, and strengthening the economy.
Sources: ABC News, PBS News

New Jersey Mikie Sherrill HISTORIC

Result: Democrat Mikie Sherrill defeated Republican Jack Ciattarelli by 13 points (56.2% to 43.2%), in a race that was widely expected to be close.
Why It's Important: New Jersey has never elected a governor from the same party in three cycles in a row, making this a historic achievement. Sherrill won by running a campaign focused largely on the economy, public safety, and health care. The victory demonstrated strong voter rejection of Trump's agenda, as Republican Jack Ciattarelli had vocally embraced Trump's support despite the president's low approval ratings.
Sources: CBS News, LiveNOW from FOX
üåÜ Historic Mayoral Victory

New York City Zohran Mamdani HISTORIC

Result: Democratic socialist Zohran Mamdani won the New York mayoral election with more than 1 million votes (50.4%), defeating independent Andrew Cuomo (41.6%) and Republican Curtis Sliwa (7.1%) to become the city's first Muslim mayor.
Why It's Important: More than 2 million New Yorkers cast ballots, the first time a mayoral election crossed that threshold since 1969. By the time 91% of votes were counted, Mamdani's tally of 1,036,051 votes was already the highest any New York mayoral candidate has won since 1965 (when Republican John Lindsay secured 1,149,106 votes). The 34-year-old will become the youngest NYC mayor in more than a century. Mamdani addressed Trump directly during his victory speech after Trump threatened to withhold federal funding from New York if Mamdani won, stating: "To get to any of us, you will have to get through all of us."
Sources: Al Jazeera, ABC News

Pennsylvania Three Democratic Justices Retained

Result: Pennsylvania voters approved the retention of three state Supreme Court justices - Christine Donohue, Kevin Dougherty, and David Wecht - preserving Democrats' 5-2 majority on the battleground state's high court.
Why It's Important: The Democratic-controlled court struck down a GOP-drawn congressional map in 2018 and upheld a mail voting law four years later. The court could again be called on to settle partisan battles over election laws ahead of the 2026 midterm contests to decide the governor's office and the U.S. House majority. The races drew over $14 million in campaign spending, with millions from Republican billionaire Jeff Yass trying to reshape the court's politics. President Trump urged Pennsylvanians to vote "NO, NO, NO" on the liberal justices, but voters overwhelmingly chose to retain them.
Sources: NBC News, Wikipedia
‚ö° Georgia Public Service Commission Breakthrough

Georgia Peter Hubbard & Alicia Johnson HISTORIC

Result: Democrats Peter Hubbard and Alicia Johnson delivered an upset, defeating two Republican incumbents on Georgia's Public Service Commission with approximately 60% of the vote each.
Why It's Important: This marks the first time the Democratic Party has won a statewide constitutional office in Georgia since 2006 - nearly 20 years. Johnson also became the first African-American woman on the PSC, as well as the first African American woman ever elected to statewide office in Georgia. The PSC regulates Georgia's major utilities, including Georgia Power, and its decisions directly impact residential energy bills. Voters were frustrated by soaring power costs, with 3 in 4 Americans concerned about rising utility bills. Both candidates ran on platforms to lower utility costs and increase accountability.
Sources: Georgia Recorder, Wikipedia

Mississippi Breaking the GOP Supermajority HISTORIC

Result: Democrats broke the Republican Party's supermajority in the Mississippi Senate after six years. Voters elected Democrats to two seats previously held by Republicans (Theresa Gillespie Isom in District 2 and Johnny DuPree in District 45), reducing GOP senators from 36 to 34. In the House, Democrat Justin Crosby flipped House District 22, defeating incumbent Republican Jon Lancaster.
Why It's Important: While Republicans will still control the legislative agenda, Democrats can now prevent Republican leadership from reviving legislation that had died on key deadlines or block Republicans from amending parts of the state constitution. The victories followed special legislative elections after a federal court ordered the state to redraw 14 total House and Senate districts, finding the maps drawn in 2022 discriminated against Black voters. The Mississippi Democratic Party called the victory "a historic rebuke of extremism," with chairman Cheikh Taylor stating "Mississippi is no longer a foregone conclusion‚Äîwe are a battleground state."
Sources: Mississippi Free Press, Mississippi Today

California Proposition 50 Passes

Result: California voters approved Proposition 50 with nearly 64% support, which will replace lines drawn by an independent commission with a map that could net Democrats as many as five new seats in the 2026 U.S. House elections.
Why It's Important: The measure was introduced in response to President Trump's push in Republican-led states like Texas to redraw district maps to favor Republicans as the GOP looks to shore up or expand its narrow House majority. The new lines will be in place through the 2030 election and could be crucial for Democrats' efforts to win control of the US House next year. Governor Gavin Newsom framed it as necessary to "fight fire with fire" and called on other Democratic-led states like Virginia, Maryland, New York, Illinois, and Colorado to follow suit. Newsom's campaign raised over $100 million for the measure, with support from House Majority PAC and George Soros' Fund for Policy Reform.
Sources: NPR, CNN

Pennsylvania County-Level Wins in Bellwether Counties

Result: Democrats swept the top "row offices" in purple-hued Bucks County, electing the county's first-ever Democratic district attorney and defeating an incumbent Republican sheriff a year after Trump narrowly won there. Democrats similarly notched commanding victories in county executive races in Erie, Lehigh, and Northampton counties‚Äîall bellwether counties in recent presidential elections.
Why It's Important: These county-level victories in swing areas demonstrate Democratic strength in regions that often predict statewide and national trends. Winning in counties Trump carried just a year earlier shows significant momentum shift and suggests potential Democratic advantages in the 2026 midterms.
Sources: NPR

Virginia Attorney General Race - Jay Jones

Result: Democrat Jay Jones won the Virginia attorney general's race, even after text messages surfaced weeks before Election Day in which he depicted the murder of political opponents.
Why It's Important: The victory was significant as a sign of the extent of GOP struggles, with even a flawed Democratic candidate winning in what would typically be a competitive race. It demonstrated the strong anti-Trump sentiment among Virginia voters.
Sources: PBS News

Virginia Lieutenant Governor - Ghazala Hashmi HISTORIC

Result: Democrat Ghazala Hashmi was elected as Virginia's Lieutenant Governor.
Why It's Important: Hashmi made history as the first Muslim American elected to this office in Virginia, joining Zohran Mamdani as one of two Muslim Americans elected to major offices on the same night.
Sources: ABC News

üìå Overall Significance & Key Takeaways

Voter Sentiment: The results were seen as voters registering their displeasure with Trump and congressional Republicans' agenda, particularly on the economy and affordability, which continued to be top of mind for voters. About 6 in 10 voters in Virginia and New Jersey said they are "angry" or "dissatisfied" with the way things are going in the country today under Trump's leadership.

Economic Focus: Democratic candidates who focused on affordability, lowering costs, and economic issues performed strongly. Exit polls showed the economy was the most important issue across all races, with voters concerned about the cost of living.

Trump Factor: GOP candidates who closely aligned themselves with President Trump struggled significantly, even in traditionally Republican areas. Trump was largely absent from the campaign trail but posted on social media claiming "TRUMP WASN'T ON THE BALLOT" as a reason for Republican losses.

Looking Ahead: Former President Barack Obama stated that the victories signaled a turning point for the party: "It's a reminder that when we come together around strong, forward-looking leaders who care about the issues that matter, we can win." These results provide Democrats with significant momentum heading into the critical 2026 midterm elections.
