House Republican efforts to impose Medicaid work requirements and per-capita caps were covered by WSJ and National Review as long-overdue structural reforms to a program that had grown far beyond its original scope. National Review argued work requirements had bipartisan support in principle and pointed to successful state-level experiments. The Dispatch flagged that several Republican governors from expansion states were publicly opposing the cuts, creating potential intra-party complications for the reconciliation bill.

NIH "Ideological Review" — Accountability or Overreach?
The administration's freeze of NIH grants pending "ideological review" generated debate even within conservative circles. Breitbart defended the freeze as long-overdue accountability for a bureaucracy that had funded ideologically driven research. National Review and The Dispatch were more critical, warning that freezing cancer and Alzheimer's research had no defensible public health justification and risked eroding public trust in genuine conservative critiques of federal science funding.

Cuba Oil Tariff EO: Maximum Pressure Expands
Trump's executive order imposing 25% tariffs on countries purchasing Cuban crude was covered by Breitbart and NY Post as a logical extension of maximum-pressure doctrine to the Western Hemisphere. Cuban-American conservatives praised the measure. Venezuela's pivot to Chinese and Russian payment mechanisms was framed as further evidence that the adversarial axis the administration had identified — Tehran-Havana-Caracas — was real and required sustained economic pressure.

Canada Pivots Toward China: Ally Betrayal?
Reports that Canada was exploring expanded trade frameworks with China in response to US tariff pressure were covered by National Review and The Dispatch as a troubling sign that USMCA partners were using tariff tensions as cover for strategic realignment with Beijing. Both outlets argued Carney's government was making a short-sighted economic bet that would compromise Five Eyes intelligence relationships and broader Western alliance cohesion at a critical moment.

USMCA Review: Renegotiating the Terms
The administration's triggering of early USMCA review provisions was covered by the WSJ as a high-stakes gamble that could rewrite North American trade relationships. National Review supported the pressure on Canada and Mexico to accept stronger enforcement provisions on digital trade, dairy, and auto content — but warned that the Iran war had consumed White House bandwidth at precisely the moment when sustained trade negotiating focus was needed.
