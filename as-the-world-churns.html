Kristi Noem
Banished to the Shield of the Americas
Kristi sits today in the strange purgatory of a title that nobody can fully explain — Special Envoy to the Shield of the Americas — nursing wounds that multiply even now she is gone. Will Kristi find peace in the Shield of the Americas — whatever that is — or does the Don have further plans for a woman who knows where certain bodies are buried? “Last Call at the Department of Labor”
● Story Ongoing
Lori Chavez-DeRemer
Secretary of Labor — Until Very Recently
At this very moment, somewhere in Washington, the investigation continues. It has not concluded. It lingers, as these things do, over the ruins of a Cabinet career that lasted barely long enough for the stationery to arrive. Lori is gone — “resigned,” the White House says, with the careful enunciation of an institution that has learned to choose its words — but the story she leaves behind is still very much in progress. Her husband — her own husband — has been formally and officially banned from the premises of the Department of Labor, following allegations of misconduct against female staff members of such gravity that a District of Columbia police investigation was opened, conducted, and inconveniently closed without a resolution that satisfies anyone. Two of Lori’s own aides have already resigned mid-investigation, their nerves having reached their natural limit. And hanging over all of this is the question of the after-hours refreshments — the allegation that Lori herself was not always in the condition that her office required. She departs under a cloud so dense and so specifically detailed that calling it a resignation requires a certain commitment to the euphemism. The investigation, meanwhile, does not share her calendar. The inquiry is open. The husband remains banned from the building. And somewhere in Washington, two former aides are deciding whether or not to talk.

“The Silent Man and His Silent Exit”
● Story Unclear
John Phelan
Former Secretary of the Navy — Gone Since Wednesday
He is gone. The Pentagon announced it with two words — effective immediately — and then said nothing further. John Phelan, a man of banking and not of battle, who navigated the treacherous waters of the Pentagon for over a year without generating a single headline, has vanished from public life as quietly and as completely as a ship dropping below the horizon. Washington does not know what to make of this. In a season of spectacular, lurid, well-documented crashes — affairs and fetishes and banned husbands and misaddressed group chats — the quiet departure is somehow the most disquieting of all. There is no scandal on record. No congressional testimony. No tabloid photograph. No Truth Social post of farewell. He was there. Now he is not. Why? The capital, which feeds on information and starves on silence, finds itself in the unfamiliar position of having nothing to say. It is not a condition Washington wears gracefully. What did John Phelan know? What did he see? And why, of all the departures of this tumultuous season, is his the one that nobody will explain?

“The Chat That Will Not Die”
● The Repercussions Continue
Mike Waltz
Former National Security Advisor — Now Ambassador-Designate to the UN
The Signal app has not been removed from official Washington’s phones. It sits there still, on the devices of the powerful, a small and devastating rectangle of infinite potential. Mike Waltz knows this better than anyone alive, for it was his finger — his own finger, on his own phone — that added the editor-in-chief of The Atlantic to a group chat in which cabinet members were calmly discussing military strikes on Yemen. Jeffrey Goldberg read every word of it. Then he wrote every word of it. The story erupted across the republic like a depth charge. The Don, for whom the appearance of competence is not optional, was not able to locate the silver lining. And Mike was gone — only to reappear, in Washington’s most durable tradition, as the Ambassador-Designate to the United Nations. In this city, no one ever truly disappears. They are merely reassigned. But the episode refuses to be forgotten. The chat still echoes. Other chats have since come to light. The culture of careless encryption is alive and well — and it is currently employed by the Secretary of Defense. Will Mike’s UN appointment sail through the Senate, or will certain senators wish to discuss, at considerable length, that particular Thursday on Signal?

“The Richest Ghost in Washington”
● Departed, Yet Somehow Present
Elon Musk
Former Special Government Employee — Still Technically Everything Else
He is gone from the building, yet he is everywhere. DOGE — his creature, his instrument, his gift to a grateful or horrified republic, depending entirely on one’s position within it — continues its work without him, a chainsaw that runs unattended. The agencies he targeted are diminished. The employees he found redundant are, in many cases, still pursuing legal remedies. And Elon himself has returned to his rockets, his automobiles, and the social media platform that he owns and on which the national conversation now, improbably, takes place. His co-architect Vivek Ramaswamy departed in a blaze of philosophical incompatibility even earlier, finding that the ideological space between himself and the rest of the administration was wider than the org chart had suggested. Between them, Elon and Vivek reshaped the federal government in ways that will be argued about in seminars for decades. Whether those arguments will take place in adequately funded institutions remains an open question. He announced his “scheduled time” was concluded with the air of a man who had accomplished what he came to accomplish. Washington is quieter in his absence. It is not, and may never again be, quite the same. DOGE runs on. The lawsuits accumulate. And Elon, from his distance, still posts. Does the Don still take his calls?
